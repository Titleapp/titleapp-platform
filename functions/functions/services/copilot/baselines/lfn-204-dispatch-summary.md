## LFN.204.21 -- Standby and Flight or Ground Request Guidelines

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Origination Date | 02/06 |
| Last Revision Date | 04/23 |
| Last Review Date | 04/23 |
| Next Review Date | 03/24 |

### PURPOSE

Our goal is to have aircraft airborne and ambulances enroute in the shortest, safest, and most expeditious manner possible. The times listed below are guidelines only. Nothing in this policy will be construed to imply 'shortcuts' can or should be taken. The pilot is the final authority regarding aircraft operations and will not be rushed for any reason or compromise safety in the name of speed. Weather checks, adding fuel, refilling oxygen, and maintenance are just some of the reasons a flight request can be delayed.

## LFN.204.42 -- Disaster Response

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Origination Date | 02/14 |
| Last Revision Date | 04/23 |
| Last Review Date | 04/23 |
| Next Review Date | 03/24 |

### PURPOSE

LFN has multiple aircraft and ground ambulances capable of being mobilized in a disaster. No policy or procedure can account for every type of disaster; however, this policy provides a foundation of procedures to follow during a natural disaster, terrorist event, pandemic, weapons of mass destruction event, or other disaster involving multiple victims. LFN is integrated with numerous local and regional disaster plans, and regularly participates in mass casualty drills. All personnel must be familiar with this plan.

---

## LFN.204.43 -- Ground Critical Care Transport (CCT) Options

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Origination Date | 01/16 |
| Last Revision Date | 05/23 |
| Last Review Date | 05/23 |
| Next Review Date | 03/24 |

### PURPOSE

LFN is a critical care resource to area hospitals and EMS agencies. When weather precludes flight of a patient, facilities still rely on LFN for transport options. This policy defines how the LFN Communications Center and medical crews will conduct ground CCT transports for areas not readily served by LFN ground ambulances. In some instances, LFN may have an agreement with local EMS.
