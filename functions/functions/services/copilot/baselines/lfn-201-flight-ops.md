## LFN.201.01 -- MEDICAL SUPERVISION PLAN (MSP)

**Category:** PATIENT CARE GUIDELINES
**Policy #:** LFN.201.01
**Origination Date:** 05/13
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

The MSP is a document required by Idaho EMS that describes the process in which medical supervision is given to an Idaho agency's EMS providers. This policy describes the process in which the MSP is reviewed, updated, and submitted to the state of Idaho.

### POLICY

1. Attachment A, Life Flight Network's most current MSP, will be reviewed at least annually to ensure its contents comply with the Idaho EMS MSP Development Guide (published by Idaho Department of Health & Welfare) and Life Flight Network's (LFN) Policies and Procedures.

2. The LFN Medical Director and Chief Clinical Officer (CCO) will be responsible for reviewing the contents, making any necessary updates, and submitting to the Idaho Department of Health & Welfare and applicable agencies as necessary.

3. The MSP is only applicable to the state of Idaho. Any conflict between the MSP and other LFN policies and procedures is unintentional. The MSP is subordinate to other LFN policies and procedures.

## LFN.201.05 -- CLINICAL STAFFING

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.05
**Origination Date:** 05/13
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 2 of 2

### PURPOSE

To define clinical staffing levels and the minimum qualifications necessary for clinical staff to transport patients.

### POLICY

1. All LFN clinical bases will be staffed with a minimum of two medical crew members at all times:
   a. A Nurse (RN) and a Paramedic (PM), OR
   b. A Nurse (RN) and a Respiratory Therapist (RT), OR
   c. A Nurse (RN) and an EMT, OR
   d. Two Nurses, OR
   e. A Paramedic and an EMT (BLS Ground only), OR
   f. Two Paramedics (BLS Ground only), OR
   g. Two EMTs (BLS Ground only)

2. If a base falls below minimum clinical staffing levels, the Clinical Manager or designee will be notified immediately. All efforts will be made to maintain staffing. If staffing cannot be maintained, the base may need to be taken out of service.

3. The Clinical Administrator On Call (AOC) will be notified if a base is taken out of service due to staffing.

4. All LFN clinical staff must maintain a current and valid state license and/or certification as applicable to their scope of practice and the states in which LFN operates.

5. All clinical staff must complete the LFN orientation and training program prior to functioning independently.

6. Clinical staff that are licensed and/or certified in a state that LFN does not operate in must obtain reciprocity or a license/certification in at least one state that LFN operates in.

7. Single Medical Attendant (SMA) Transports:
   a. SMA transports may be completed when one crew member is out of service for a period of time that is not expected to last the duration of the shift.
   b. The on-duty crew member, CM and/or AOC will make the determination to complete SMA transports.
   c. Only Nurses or Paramedics who have been released from orientation can complete SMA transports.
   d. SMA transports will be limited to interfacility transports that meet BLS or ALS criteria.
   e. The single medical attendant will make the determination of the medical appropriateness of completing the transport.

### PURPOSE

To outline the procedure for obtaining physician medical direction.

### POLICY

1. Physician Medical Direction is available to LFN crews 24 hours per day, seven days per week through the LFN Medical Director or the on-call physician.

2. Medical crews shall contact physician medical direction in the following situations:
   a. When patient care needs exceed the scope of the LFN Patient Care Guidelines.
   b. When the crew feels the need for additional medical guidance.
   c. When the patient's condition warrants consultation.

3. Medical crews may contact physician medical direction through the LFN Communications Center.

4. In the event physician medical direction cannot be reached through the LFN Communications Center, the medical crew should contact the sending or receiving physician for guidance.

5. All physician medical direction contacts shall be documented in the patient care record.

## LFN.201.07 -- REFUSAL OF TREATMENT

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.07
**Origination Date:** 05/13
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

To provide guidance when a patient or their representative refuses treatment and/or transport.

### POLICY

1. A competent adult patient has the right to refuse treatment and/or transport.

2. In the event of a patient refusal, the medical crew will:
   a. Assess the patient's decision-making capacity.
   b. Ensure the patient understands the potential risks associated with refusing treatment and/or transport.
   c. Make every reasonable attempt to convince the patient to accept treatment and/or transport.
   d. Contact physician medical direction for guidance.
   e. Document the refusal thoroughly in the patient care record.
   f. Complete the "Patient Refusal" form.

3. If the patient is a minor, every effort will be made to obtain consent from a parent or legal guardian before honoring the refusal.

4. The Clinical Administrator On Call (AOC) or Shift Supervisor will be notified of all refusals.

## LFN.201.10 -- CRITERIA FOR TRANSPORT ACCEPTANCE

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.10
**Origination Date:** 02/14
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

To define the criteria for accepting or declining transport requests.

### POLICY

1. LFN will accept transport requests that are within the scope and capability of the organization.

2. The Communications Specialist will gather initial information regarding the transport request, including patient acuity, sending and receiving facilities, and any special needs.

3. The medical crew will make the final determination on the medical appropriateness of the transport after reviewing available information.

4. Transports may be declined in the following situations:
   a. The patient's condition exceeds the scope of the LFN Patient Care Guidelines and appropriate resources are not available.
   b. Weather or aircraft limitations prevent safe transport.
   c. The transport does not fall within LFN's service capabilities.

5. All declined transports will be documented and the Clinical Administrator On Call (AOC) will be notified.

## LFN.201.11 -- AIR TO GROUND CONVERSION

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.11
**Origination Date:** 02/14
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

To define the procedure for converting an air transport to a ground transport.

### POLICY

1. The decision to convert from air to ground transport may be made at any point during the transport process.

2. Reasons for conversion may include but are not limited to:
   a. Deteriorating weather conditions
   b. Aircraft mechanical issues
   c. Patient condition changes
   d. Weight and balance limitations

3. The pilot has final authority on all aviation-related decisions.

4. The medical crew has final authority on all patient care-related decisions.

5. When a conversion occurs, the Communications Center will coordinate ground transport resources.

6. All conversions will be documented in the patient care record and an appropriate report completed.

## LFN.201.13 -- MEDICAL CREW SCOPE OF PRACTICE

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.13
**Origination Date:** 02/14
**Last Revision Date:** 07/22
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 2 of 2

### PURPOSE

To define the scope of practice for LFN medical crew members.

### POLICY

1. All LFN medical crew members will function within the scope of their state license and/or certification and within the scope of the LFN Patient Care Guidelines.

2. Nurses (RN) will practice under the authority of their state nursing license and applicable nurse practice act.

3. Paramedics will practice under the medical direction of the LFN Medical Director and within the scope of their state certification/license.

4. Respiratory Therapists will practice under the authority of their state license and applicable practice act.

5. EMTs will practice under the medical direction of the LFN Medical Director and within the scope of their state certification.

6. All LFN medical crew members may perform skills and administer medications as outlined in the LFN Patient Care Guidelines, provided the skill or medication is within their individual scope of practice.

7. In the event of a conflict between state scope of practice and LFN Patient Care Guidelines, the more restrictive standard will apply.

8. Student riders or observers may not provide direct patient care.

## LFN.201.27 -- OHSU (MRH) NOTIFICATION

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.27
**Origination Date:** 03/12
**Last Revision Date:** 07/22
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

To outline a procedure for contacting Medical Resource Hospital (MRH) to obtain the appropriate receiving trauma center for trauma scene calls occurring in Region 1, Region 2, ATAB 1, or where the destination will be a Portland trauma center.

### POLICY

1. Upon activation of a scene request for a traumatic injury where the patient will likely be transported to Legacy Emanuel or OHSU, the LFN crew will contact MRH via phone or radio and request a destination hospital. The destination hospital rotates between the two level 1 trauma centers, OHSU and Legacy Emanuel.

### PURPOSE

A patient may be placed on a mental health hold(1) by either a peace officer(2) or a qualified licensed practitioner.(3) LFN's objective is to safely transport a patient placed on a mental health hold.

### POLICY

1. The Sending Physician shall issue an order applicable to the inter-facility transport.
2. Patients must be dressed only in a hospital gown and undergarments prior to transport to ensure safety.
3. Patient possessions must be secured in a patient belongings bag and stowed away from the patient care area.
4. For the safety of the patient and others, and to decrease the risk of elopement, the patient must be secured to the gurney within the sending facility and must remain secured for the duration of the transport unless it becomes necessary to unsecure the patient to provide treatment.
5. Physical or chemical restraint must be applied consistent with the sending provider order or, if the order does not contain instructions regarding the use of restraints, restraints may be applied consistent with LFN Patient Care Guidelines.
6. If, after assessment of the patient and prior to transport, LFN personnel determine they will not be able to safely transport the patient, including retaining safe control of the patient using the above methods, LFN personnel may decline the transport. In this circumstance, the Clinical Administrator On Call must be consulted.

**Footnotes:**
1. This category of patients is described in Idaho Statute 66-326.
2. The term "peace officer" includes state probation and parole officers exercising their authority to supervise probationers and parolees. See Idaho Statute 66-326.
3. The term "qualified licensed practitioner" includes a physician medical staff member of the hospital at which the person presented or was brought to receive medical or mental health care, and a physician's assistant and advanced practice registered nurse practicing in such hospital. See Idaho Statute 66-326.

## LFN.201.33 -- i-STAT QUALITY CONTROL, PROFICIENCY TESTING, & CALIBRATION VERIFICATION

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS

### PURPOSE

To monitor the quality of the pre-analytical, analytical, and post-analytical processes to ensure accurate and reliable patient testing results.

### POLICY

**A. Quality Control**
Quality control requirements are incorporated into the i-STAT procedure. An Individualized Quality Control Plan (IQCP) has been developed to assess risk and define practices that will be used to mitigate that risk. Refer to the IQCP for full details of the Quality Control Plan.

**B. Proficiency Testing**
It is the policy of LFN to participate in an external audit system to verify the analytical procedures and assure analytical accuracy. LFN is enrolled in the CAP surveys program for proficiency testing for each regulated analyte.

Interlaboratory communication about proficiency testing samples before submission of data to the proficiency testing provider, or referral of proficiency testing specimens to an outside laboratory are prohibited.

Personnel who routinely perform testing on the i-STAT test the proficiency testing samples as they would a patient specimen. Results are recorded along with the name of the person performing the test. The surveys are rotated among all staff performing testing on the i-STAT. All samples in each survey are run on one analyzer. The surveys are rotated among all analyzers in use.

Upon receipt of the evaluation of proficiency testing results from CAP, the Laboratory Point of Care Specialist reviews results and performs an investigation for any results that are unacceptable or demonstrate bias. A Proficiency Testing Review and Investigation Report is completed for each survey with investigation and corrective actions documented for any unacceptable results or bias. The Medical Test Site Director reviews and signs all Review and Investigation Reports. All unacceptable results are discussed with the staff that performed the survey.

The proficiency testing records are maintained in the i-STAT CAP file electronically. The Life Flight Technical Consultant is responsible for maintaining these records for two years.

**C. CLEW and Jam Software Updates**
The iSTAT software must be updated twice a year in June and December. The CLEW software and JAMS application software is updated in each analyzer when the new versions are delivered by the vendor. The analyzers lock up if the software version expires and patient testing can not be performed.

**D. Calibration Verification**
Calibration verification is performed by testing materials of known concentration in the same manner as patient samples to ensure the test system is accurately measuring values throughout the reportable range. Materials of known concentrations are ordered from Abbott. Three levels must be tested; one at the high end of the reportable range, one at the low end of the reportable range and one near the midpoint of the reportable range.

Calibration verification is performed by LFN personnel in June and December each year immediately after each CLEW and JAMS software update. They should also be performed whenever any of the following occur:
1. All of the reagents used for a test procedure are changed to new lot numbers, unless LFN can demonstrate that changing reagent lot numbers does not affect the range used to report patient tests results and control values are not adversely affected by reagent lot number changes.
2. When there is a major preventive maintenance, all replacement analyzers will be verified by reportable range accuracy and precision.
3. If control materials reflect an unusual trend or shift, are outside of LFN's acceptable limits, and other means of assessing and correcting are unacceptable.
4. Control values fail to identify and correct the problem.

**E. Oversite**
Supervision of the quality control, proficiency testing and calibration verification includes the following:
1. Overall review is the responsibility of the Medical Test Site Director.
2. Ongoing review is delegated to the SHMC Point of Care Specialist.
3. The SHMC Point of Care Specialist and Life Flight Technical Consultant will review the monthly electronic quality control and quality control performed on each new lot of cartridges.
4. Policy and procedure review will be done biannually by the SHMC Point of Care Specialist. Major revisions must be approved by the Medical Test Site Director.
5. Review of Proficiency Testing challenge attestation page is delegated to SHMC Point of Care Specialist or Life Flight Technical Consultant prior to submission.

**F. Moderately Complex Tests**
For moderately complex tests such as the i-Stat, IQCP will be followed. IQCP consists of three elements:
1. Risk Assessment (RA)
2. Quality Control Plan (QCP)
3. Quality Assessment (QA)

These components will be reviewed on at least an annual basis. The Laboratory Director needs to approve and sign this document. See IQCP.

Attachments:
- Attachment 1: Laboratory Delegation
- Attachment 2: IQCP Risk Assessment (I-Stat Cartridge EG6+, 5 pages covering Pre-Analytic, Analytic, Post-Analytic risk assessments)

**Attachment 1 -- Laboratory Delegation:**
The Laboratory Director has authority and responsibility for the overall technical operation, clinical operation, and administration of the laboratory. They must implement and maintain the standards mandated by state and federal regulations to assure quality patient care. Onsite visits need not be daily, if the Director is available by telephone, email, or other immediate response mechanism.

The Laboratory Director has delegated responsibilities to others to act on their behalf on specific duties, such as competency assessment. Those individuals authorized to act on their behalf are the technical Consultant at Life Flight Network. Delegation does not mean abdication. Life Flight Technical Consultant will bring to the attention of the Laboratory Director any unusual occurrence, incident report related to quality, quality assurance/quality improvement reports and peer review.

## LFN.201.36 -- VERIFICATION OF NEW OR REPLACEMENT i-STATS

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS

**Origination Date:** 06/16
**Last Revision Date:** 05/17
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24

### PURPOSE

To outline the verification process for new or replacement i-STAT analyzers. To comply with CLIA and the accrediting organization's requirements, each new or replacement handheld unit must be verified before being put into use. CLIA and the accrediting organization define what needs to be verified, but do not detail a procedure for the verification.

### POLICY

Abbot Point-of-Care recommends the following minimum data be collected:

1. **Reportable Range:** Test the lowest level, the mid-level and highest level of calibration samples available for each test performed on a new or replacement handheld i-STAT unit. Results must be within the acceptable range(s) on the *Value Assignment Sheets*. Store the *Value Assignment Sheets* with the data as evidence the results were within acceptable limits.

2. **Accuracy:** Use the data from the Reportable Range study to assess accuracy. In addition, test one or more employee samples on the new or replacement handheld and a previously verified handheld. The difference(s) between the new or replacement should not exceed the required level of agreement between systems. LFN uses CAP's Allowable Error Limits. All data must be kept for the life of analyzer.

3. **Precision:** Test levels 1 and 3 of the appropriate controls will be run in duplicate on two consecutive days on any new or replacement handheld unit. Each analyte will be measured using a new or replacement handheld unit and must be included in the *Precision* study. *Value Assignment Sheets* will be stored with the data as evidence results were within acceptable limits.

4. **Reference Range:** Use the reference range established at the time of initial verification.

**Policy #:** LFN.201.38
**Origination Date:** 08/16
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24

### PURPOSE

To outline the procedure for performing point-of-care testing utilizing the i-STAT Portable Clinical Analyzer.

### POLICY

The i-STAT System incorporates comprehensive components needed to perform blood analysis at the point of care. The System consists of the following primary components:

- **Analyzers:** Analyzers can be the i-STAT Portable Clinical Analyzer, the i-STAT1 Analyzer, or the Blood Analysis Module, which is used in conjunction with the Philips Medical Systems (formerly Agilent Technologies) CMS and 24/26 Patient Monitor. When a sample-filled i-STAT cartridge is inserted into an analyzer for analysis, the analyzer automatically controls all functions of the testing cycle including fluid movement within the cartridge, calibration, and continuous quality monitoring.

- **Cartridges:** A single-use disposable cartridge contains microfabricated sensors, a calibrant solution, fluidics system, and a waste chamber. Sensors for analysis of pH, PCO2, PO2, sodium, potassium, chloride, ionized calcium, glucose, lactate, creatinine, blood urea nitrogen (BUN) and hematocrit are available in a variety of panel configurations. Cartridges are also available for Celite-ACT, Kaolin-ACT, PT/INR, and Troponin I/cTnI (Table 1). A whole blood sample of approximately 1 to 3 drops is dispensed into the cartridge sample well, and the sample well is sealed before inserting it into the analyzer.

**I. SUPPLIES AND STORAGE REQUIREMENTS**

A. **Cartridges:** Stored at 2 to 8 degrees C (35 to 46 degrees F). **Do not allow cartridges to freeze.** May be stored at room temperature (18 to 30 degrees C or 64 to 86 degrees F) for two months. Cartridges should not be returned to the refrigerator once at room temperature and should not be exposed to temperatures above 30 degrees C (86 degrees F). If the pouch has been punctured, the cartridge should not be used. Write the date on the cartridge box or individual cartridge pouches to indicate the two-month room temperature expiration date. Do not use expired Cartridges. **For acceptance of newly received cartridge lots, check the Temperature Monitor and perform integrity testing.**

B. **Check Temperature Monitor:** i-STAT cartridges are shipped refrigerated. Action: Fill out the record of receipt and forward materials to refrigerator.

C. **Controls:** *i-STAT Tri-Controls, Level 1 and 3, for blood gases, electrolytes, chemistries, hematocrit.* Store at 2 to 8 degrees C (35 to 46 degrees F). Controls may be stored at room temperature (18 to 30 degrees C or 64 to 86 degrees F) for five days. Do not use after expiration date on the box and ampules.

**II. BLOOD SPECIMENS**

A. **Blood Collection Equipment**
1. *Cartridges for Blood Gas/Electrolytes/Chemistries/Hematocrit:*
   - Skin puncture: lancet and capillary collection tube (plain, lithium heparin, or balanced heparin)
   - Venipuncture: lithium or sodium heparin collection tubes and disposable transfer device (e.g., 1cc syringe and a 16-to-20-gauge needle)
   - Arterial puncture: Plain syringe or blood gas syringe with heparin and labeled for the assays performed or with the least amount of heparin to prevent clotting (10 U heparin/mL of blood)

2. *Suitable Specimens for Cartridges for Blood Gases, Electrolytes, Chemistries, and Hematocrit:*
   - Fresh whole blood in a plain capillary collection tube or capillary collection tube with balanced heparin. Test within 3 minutes.
   - Fresh whole blood in a collection tube with lithium or sodium heparin anticoagulant. Fill collection tubes to capacity. Test within 10 minutes.
   - Fresh whole blood in a plain plastic syringe or in a blood gas syringe labeled for the assays to be performed. Fill syringes for correct blood-to-heparin ratio. Test within 3 minutes.

3. *Finger and Heel-stick Specimens:* For tests other than PT/INR, wipe away the first drop of blood, which contains excess tissue fluid which can increase the potassium result and decrease other test results. Avoid drawing air into the capillary tube. Use balanced heparin or plain capillary tubes for ionized calcium. Test samples immediately to avoid clotting (especially in neonates).

4. *Criteria For Specimen Rejection:*
   - Evidence of clotting
   - Specimens collected in vacuum tubes with anticoagulant other than lithium or sodium heparin
   - Syringe for pH, PCO2, and PO2 with air bubbles in sample
   - Incompletely filled vacuum tube for the measurement of PCO2

5. *Precautions: Avoid the Following Circumstances:*
   - Drawing a specimen from an arm with an I.V.
   - Stasis (tourniquet left on longer than one minute before venipuncture)
   - Extra muscle activity (fist pumping)
   - Hemolysis (alcohol left over puncture site, or a traumatic draw)
   - Icing before filling cartridge
   - Time delays before filling cartridge, especially lactate, ACT, and PT/INR
   - Exposing the sample to air when measuring pH, PCO2, and PO2

**B. PROCEDURE FOR ANALYSIS**

1. *Preparation for Use:* An individual cartridge may be used after standing 5 minutes, in its pouch, at room temperature. An entire box should stand at room temperature for one hour before cartridges are used.

2. *Procedure for Cartridge Testing (other than PT/INR or Troponin I/cTnI):*
   a-k. Detailed step-by-step procedure for cartridge testing, including:
   - Remove cartridge from pouch, avoiding touching contact pads
   - Mix sample thoroughly, dispense blood into sample well to fill mark
   - Close cover, insert cartridge, enter operator ID (last 6 digits of LFN employee number)
   - **Enter the patient ID number, use the last 7 digits of the TC number (20-11111)**
   - Select tests, enter additional parameters (temperature, FIO2, ventilator settings)
   - View results on display screen

3. *Reference Ranges, Reportable Ranges, and Test Unit Conversions* (detailed table with arterial and venous reference ranges for: Sodium, Potassium, Chloride, BUN, UREA, Glucose, Creatinine, Ionized Calcium, pH, PCO2, PO2, Hematocrit, Lactate, HCO3, TCO2, BEecf, Anion Gap, sO2, Hb)

4. *Documenting and Reporting Results:* Document results in the patient electronic record; review, evaluate and correlate critical values to patient condition. Report critical values to the appropriate personnel (referring/receiving physicians or designees). Appropriate PRN interventions to correct critical lab values will be initiated as per physician-driven Life Flight Network protocols.

**III. QUALITY CONTROL**

A. **Daily Procedures:**
1. *Analyzer Verification:* Verify the performance of each handheld analyzer or Blood Analysis Module every 8 hours of use using the internal or external Electronic Simulator (ES). Store at room temperature and protect contact pads from contamination. **Life Flight Network analyzers are programmed for automatic internal verification. Use an external Electronic Simulator when a second consecutive cartridge malfunctions.**
   - Detailed Action/Remedial Action procedures for PASS/FAIL results

2. *Verification of Cartridge Storage Conditions:*
   a. Refrigerated Cartridges: Verify within expiration date, refrigerator at 2 to 8 degrees C. Document in i-STAT QC Log.
   b. Room Temperature Cartridges: Verify out of refrigerator less than two months, room temperature has not exceeded 30 degrees C. Document in i-STAT QC Log.

B. **Monthly Procedures:** Print Electronic Simulator Results. Clinical Manager reviews in CDS or individual analyzer, documents review on ES Log.

C. **Integrity Testing:** From each lot of blood gas/chemistry/hematocrit cartridges received, use a representational number of cartridges to analyze i-STAT Tri Controls Level 1 and 3. Detailed procedure for testing with Tri-Control Level 1 and Level 3 (4 steps with extensive detail on ampule handling, cartridge filling, and result comparison). Remedial Action for out-of-range results.

**IV. CALIBRATION**
For cartridges, calibration is automatically performed as part of the test cycle on each cartridge type, except coagulation and immunoassay cartridges. Operator intervention is not necessary.

*Refer to main iStat 1 System Manual for detailed information on the use of the iStat analyzer*

## LFN.201.39 -- MALFUNCTIONING MEDICAL EQUIPMENT

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.39
**Origination Date:** 01/11
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 2 of 2

### PURPOSE

To identify and track all broken or otherwise failed medical equipment.

### DEFINITIONS

1. *Equipment* is defined as a medical product used in direct patient care, or in conjunction with another product or piece of equipment to provide patient care.
2. A *broken piece of medical equipment* is defined as any medical product that stops or fails to perform as specified or is broken prior to use.

### PROCEDURE

**Crew instructions:**
1. Identify and collect broken medical equipment.
2. Fill out and attach a pink "Out of Service" tag which should include date, base, complaint, and crewmember's name.
3. Complete a QM and/or OIQ service ticket.
   a. If the equipment failure had a direct effect on patient care and/or has sustained physical damage, a QM report must be filled out along with an OIQ service ticket to alert the Biomedical Manager that a replacement is needed.
   b. If a replacement piece of equipment is needed but no patient was involved and no physical damage occurred, an OIQ service ticket must be created.
   c. In order for an OIQ service ticket to be submitted the serial number of the equipment must be entered, if no serial number is apparent "NA" can be used.
4. Once the Biomedical Manager receives the OIQ service ticket, a replacement device will be sent out with a return label included in the shipping package. The Out-of-service (OOS) device will be returned to the Biomedical Manager for evaluation.
5. Contact the Clinical AOC if there was any impact to the patient.
6. The Biomedical Manager is responsible for the repair of all broken medical equipment and for communicating any unresolved issues to the appropriate manager. No other employee, including maintenance personnel, may attempt to repair medical equipment.

**Biomedical Technician Instructions:**
1. Identify defective medical equipment.
2. Evaluate device failure and repair if possible. Send to manufacturer if unable to repair in-house.
3. Determine if other products in use are at risk for similar occurrence. If so, contact the Chief Clinical Officer (CCO) for guidance.
4. Follow-up on pertinent QM and/or OIQ reports assigned to this product.

## LFN.201.41 -- CONSENT FORMS AND CERTIFICATE OF MEDICAL NECESSITY FORMS

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.41
**Origination Date:** 07/17
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 3 of 3 (note: page 2 and 3 content follows)

### PURPOSE

The Patient Consent to Transport form (consent form) is a legal document giving Life Flight Network (LFN) permission to treat and transport the patient and gives the patient a mechanism for assigning their insurance benefits, if any, to LFN. The smallest oversight can delay the billing process, therefore delaying payment. In the case of a billing dispute or treatment issue, insurance companies, patients, and their attorneys will request a copy of the consent form. An incomplete or illegible consent form can prohibit LFN from billing the patient for our services.

### POLICY

1. The patient must sign whenever possible in Section I. **If a patient signature is obtained in Section I, no other sections of the consent form need to, or should, be completed.**

2. If the patient's signature is illegible or he or she signed with an "X", the crewmember should witness the patient's signature by signing immediately below the patient signature.

3. If the patient is primary English speaking, they must sign the English consent. If primary Spanish speaking, they must sign the Spanish consent. If they speak another language, make every attempt to obtain a signature via translation services and document that on the consent form.

4. If the patient is awake and alert and refuses to sign a consent form, do not treat or transport them. A verbal consent is not sufficient when a patient is able to sign. Explain to the patient you cannot treat or transport without their signed consent and if they continue to refuse, contact the appropriate Administrator On Call (AOC) or Shift Supervisor for direction. If the patient refuses to sign and the transport is terminated, a "Patient Refusal" form will be completed according to Refusal of Treatment (LFN 201.07).

5. If the patient is incapable of signing, the crew should, at the time of service, make every effort to locate one of the authorized signers who are identified in Section II and get them to sign. Examples of reasons a patient is incapable of signing include patient is unconscious, mentally incapacitated, impaired due to alcohol or drugs, or physically restrained. The crew should look for an authorized representative in the order listed in Section II of the consent form (i.e., a legal representative is preferred over a facility representative.)

   **Steps for completing Section II of the consent form:**
   - First, document the circumstances that make it impractical for the patient to sign in Section II.
   - Next, check the box which accurately identifies the representative signer's relationship to the patient.
   - Finally, the authorized signer must sign their name and provide a printed name in Section II.

   Make sure **all** the information is complete in Section II when obtaining the signature of an authorized representative signer. **If the signature of an authorized representative (including a representative of the sending facility) is obtained on behalf of the patient in Section II, no other sections of the consent form need to, or should, be completed.**

6. Section III of the consent form should **only** be completed when the patient is physically or mentally incapable of signing in Section I **and** no authorized representative (identified in Section II) was available or willing to sign at the time of service. In such cases, the LFN crew should sign on behalf of the patient and obtain the signature of the receiving facility.

   **Steps for completing Section III of the consent form:**
   - First, document the circumstances that make it impractical for the patient to sign on the first blank line in Section III.
   - Next, document the name and address of the receiving facility and the time the ambulance services were provided.
   - Then, the LFN crew should sign in Section III and legibly print their name. This signature must be provided at the time of service.
   - Finally, the LFN crew must obtain the signature of a representative of the receiving facility at the time of transport (any representative of the receiving facility will suffice). The receiving facility representative should sign in Part B of Section III at the time the patient is delivered to the facility ("contemporaneous signature"). The facility representative should also print their name and title.
   - No modifications can be made by LFN crew once the transport is completed. Therefore, every effort must be made to ensure the consent form is completed accurately before the end of a transport.

7. If the patient is in the custody of the Department of Corrections, they still must sign their own consent form if they are able to do so. If they are not capable, the guard can sign as "Authorized Representative". If the guard refuses to sign as Authorized Representative, proceed as set forth in #5, where no family member is present.

8. The completed consent form will be attached to the Patient Care Record in the "Miscellaneous Forms, Attached Files" section. Confirmation an appropriate consent form has been attached to the Patient Care Record is part of the peer review process.

9. The Certificate of Medical Necessity (CMN) or Physician Certification Statement (PCS) must be completed and must be signed by any one of the positions listed at the bottom of the form. These positions must be representatives of the sending facility and include: MD, RN, Discharge Planner, PA, NP and CNS. Verbal or written orders given to transporting crews are not valid for purposes of the CMN; requests should be made to ensure the above-mentioned representatives' signatures are utilized to comply with EMTALA.

## LFN.201.42 -- REPORTING ABUSE OF MINORS AND VULNERABLE ADULTS

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS

**Origination Date:** 01/18
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

To outline the reporting of suspected minor and vulnerable adult abuse.

### POLICY

EMS providers in Oregon, Washington, Idaho, and Montana are mandatory reporters.

1. In the case of suspected minor abuse:
   a. Notify the Clinical or Ground Administrator On Call (AOC).
   b. Submit a Quality Management report.
   c. Reporting will occur as soon as is practical and prior to the end of shift.
   d. Reporting will follow the state guidelines for the suspected abuse for minors in the state the suspected abuse took place.
   e. Reporting guidelines can be found on the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services website, www.childwelfare.gov, "How to report child abuse or neglect."

2. In the case of suspected vulnerable adult* abuse:
   a. Notify the Clinical or Ground AOC.
   b. Submit a Quality Management report.
   c. Reporting will occur prior to the end of shift.
   d. Reporting will follow the state guidelines for suspected abuse of a vulnerable adult in the state the suspected abuse took place.
   e. Guidelines and reporting information can be found on the following websites:
      i. Washington: https://www.dshs.wa.gov/altsa/home-and-community-services/vulnerable-adult
      ii. Oregon: https://www.oregon.gov/dhs/abuse/Pages/index.aspx
      iii. Idaho: https://aging.idaho.gov/stay-safe/adult-protection/
      iv. Montana: https://dphhs.mt.gov/sltc/aps

*Vulnerable adults are persons 18 years of age or older who are unable to protect themselves from abuse, neglect, or exploitation due to physical or mental impairment which affects the person's judgment or behavior to the extent that they lack sufficient understanding or capacity to make or communicate or implement decisions.*

## LFN.201.45 -- USE OF PHYSICAL RESTRAINTS

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.45
**Origination Date:** 03/19
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

To provide guidance for the management of patients requiring physical restraints.

### POLICY

1. The goal of Life Flight Network (LFN) is to transport and appropriately treat all individuals who require our service in a safe and expeditious manner. Patient and crew safety is the number one priority.

2. Restraints should only be utilized when necessary and in situations in which the patient is exhibiting behavior that presents an immediate danger to themselves or others. Medical crew must appropriately document the reasons for the use of restraints in the patient care record.

3. Intubated patients will be adequately sedated with consideration of their medical condition. Soft wrist restraints should be considered for intubated patients to decrease the risk of self-extubation.

4. For non-intubated patients, the method of restraint must allow for adequate monitoring of pulse and respiration and should not restrict the patient or medical crew's ability to protect the airway should vomiting occur.

5. Restrained extremities will be assessed for circulation, motor function, and sensory function every fifteen (15) minutes and documented in the patient care record, along with notation of need for continued restraint use.

6. Restraint equipment applied by law enforcement (i.e., handcuffs) must provide sufficient slack to allow the patient to straighten the abdomen and chest and to take full tidal volume breaths. If applied, these devices require law enforcement continual presence to ensure patient and scene safety.

7. Non-emergent transports with soft restraints may be completed by BLS transport if the patient requires minimal control for patient or crew safety. Violent patients require ALS transport. Secure transports will not be completed.

## LFN.201.46 -- STROKE ALERT BLOOD DRAWS AT BOZEMAN HEALTH HOSPITAL

**Category:** CLINICAL
**Policy #:** LFN.201.46
**Origination Date:** 10/19
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 2 of 2

### PURPOSE

To facilitate rapid transfer of care to Bozeman Health Hospital for patients presenting with signs/symptoms of an acute stroke in need of early recognition, diagnosis, and treatment.

### POLICY

1. When possible, crews will draw labs on patients presenting with acute signs/symptoms of a stroke who are being transported to Bozeman Health Hospital.
2. All labs obtained will be drawn in flight to mitigate transport delays.
3. **DO NOT** delay patient transport or transfer of care to obtain lab draws.
4. Lab tubes will be drawn in the following order to avoid cross contamination, which may provide a false positive lab result:
   a. Light Blue -- PT/PTT/D-Dimer/INR
   b. Gold -- Extra Tube
   c. Green -- Chemistry Panel
   d. Lavender -- CBC with Differential

5. Blood for lab draws can be obtained as follows:
   a. From a new peripheral IV site:
      i. Obtain peripheral IV access, preferably with a 20G IV catheter or larger.
      ii. **DO NOT** flush the peripheral IV prior to blood draw.
      iii. Attach the vacutainer device to the peripheral IV hub.
      iv. Fill the lab tubes in correct order, Light Blue, Gold, Green, Lavender.
      v. Place an identifying wristband on the patient.
      vi. Place a sticker from the identifying name band on each tube of blood.
      vii. Initial, date, and time each tube of blood.
   b. From an existing peripheral IV:
      i. When utilizing a peripheral IV that has been previously flushed with crystalloid or medications, or has IV fluid infusing, 10 ml of waste blood MUST be drawn from the IV prior to filling lab tubes.
      ii. Pull the waste from the IV extension with a 10ml luer lock syringe and discard the blood waste syringe in a sharp's container or sharp's shuttle.
      iii. Attach the vacutainer device to the luer lock on IV extension tubing hub.
      iv. Fill the lab tubes in correct order, Light Blue, Gold, Green, Lavender.
      v. Flush the IV extension or resume the infusion to avoid clotting of the line.
      vi. Place the identifying wristband on the patient.
      vii. Place a sticker from the identifying name band on each tube of blood.
      viii. Initial, date, and time each tube of blood.

6. Upon arrival at Bozeman Health Hospital, ensure the blood drawn during transport is labeled correctly and passed off to the phlebotomist for testing.

7. Draw kits will contain the following items:
   a. 2 Alcohol Pads
   b. 1 2x2 Gauze Pad

   f. 4 Lab tubes as listed above (Light Blue, Gold, Green, Lavender)

## LFN.201.47 -- CHARTING IN BACK

**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.47
**Origination Date:** 09/20
**Last Revision Date:** 03/23
**Last Review Date:** 03/23
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 1 of 1

### PURPOSE

To clearly define times when it is and is not acceptable to chart while an aircraft is in motion.

### POLICY

Charting in back is permitted during a flight and/or ground transport under the following conditions:

1. Intent to chart in back has been briefed and agreed to by all transport team members.
2. All timely patient care has been completed and patient and/or family has been informed of intent to chart information pertinent to their care.
3. When pilot and one med crew member are actively engaged in scanning for/monitoring risk factors potentially affecting the aircraft.

Charting in back is not permitted during a flight and or ground transport if the following conditions exist:

1. Anytime a crewmember states all eyes are needed outside the aircraft and/or situational awareness of the crew has been compromised.
2. During deteriorating weather or heavy traffic conditions. If weather or traffic conditions improve significantly, intent to chart in back will be communicated to and agreed on by all transport team members.
3. During all flight within 10 miles of the intended point of takeoff or landing or as otherwise directed by the pilot. This includes start up and shutdown procedures, taxiing, take off and landings, approach and departures, and any other time as requested by the pilot in command or vehicle operator.
4. In general, charting should not take place in the presence of the patient.

## LFN.201.50 -- MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION CROSS-CHECK (MACC)


**Category:** CLINICAL OPERATIONS
**Policy #:** LFN.201.50
**Origination Date:** 06/23
**Last Revision Date:** --/--
**Last Review Date:** --/--
**Next Review Date:** 03/24
**Pages:** 3 of 3

### PURPOSE

To define the proper use of Medication Administration Cross-Check (MACC) in the LFN System

### DEFINITION

MACC is a team-based communication method to standardize the medication verification process and reduce medication errors.

### POLICY

**A. USE**

1. Safe medication administration optimally involves two LFN providers to collaborate on medication administration.
2. When two LFN providers are available, MACC will be used by nurses, paramedics, respiratory therapists, and EMTs before all medication administrations.
3. When only one LFN provider is available, such as in a single medical attendant transport, the MACC cannot be applied.
4. If Crew 1 is a paramedic or nurse and Crew 2 is an EMT, the MACC procedure should still be used with a slight alteration to the role of Crew 2. Crew 2 should visually verify the drug name, concentration, and expiration date.
5. The use of terms "Crew 1" and "Crew 2" in this policy do not reflect specific clinical disciplines. Crew 1 is the provider intending to administer the medication and Crew 2 is the provider verifying.

**B. PROCEDURE**

1. Crew 1 initiates the procedure by stating: "Medication check."
2. Crew 2 responds that they are: "Ready." It is important to avoid using ambiguous responses such as "okay" and to participate in an engaged manner.
3. Crew 1 states: "I am going to give . . ." and provides the following information:
   a. Medication name
   b. Medication dosage
   c. Administration route
   d. Administration rate
   e. Medication expiration date
   f. Patient weight
   g. Administration indication
4. If there is concurrence from Crew 2, continue the cross-check procedure. If there is no concurrence, stop and resolve any disagreement at this point.
5. If Crew 2 agrees, they will respond with the question: "Are there any contraindications?" Crew 1 must verify the patient's vital signs are appropriate for administration of the indicated medication and identify any drug allergies. Crew 1 should respond by saying, "No contraindications," or by stating and discussing the presence of any contraindications.
6. If Crew 2 concurs, they will respond with the question: "What is your volume?" or "Quantity?" for pills/tablets.
   a. For infusions, Crew 2 will respond with the question: "What is your dose, concentration, and rate?"
7. Crew 1 should state the drug concentration, the volume/rate they intend to deliver, and should show the container to Crew 2.
8. If Crew 2 agrees and makes a positive visual verification, they should respond with the phrase: "I agree; give it."

**C. CONSIDERATIONS**

1. Contraindications of medication administration should be considered. Some of these include:
   a. Verification of appropriate vital signs
   b. Known patient allergies
   c. Expiration date
   d. Patient medical history
2. If a discrepancy, disagreement, or need for clarification is encountered at any step in the process, it must be resolved prior to continuing the cross check.
3. Crew 2 can authorize the administration of the medication.
4. MACC must be completed prior to the administration of any medication when two LFN providers are available.
5. If there is an interruption or change in the patient's condition of any kind, the process must be re-initiated by Crew 1.
6. Avoid ambiguous statements or confirmations like "okay."

**D. DOCUMENTATION**

1. Use of MACC should be documented in the patient care report.

**E. REPORTING**

1. Medication administration errors will be reported to the receiving hospital, along with a Quality Management (QM) report submission in the Baldwin Safety and Compliance System.

2. Crew 1: "I am going to give." (Medication Name, Medication Dose, Administration Rate, Medication Expiration Date, Patient Weight, Administration Indication) --> Crew 2: "Are there any contraindications?"
