# RaaS — Real Estate Disclosures — Nevada (NV)

This directory defines **mandatory and standard disclosures** required for
Nevada real estate transactions under the TitleApp RaaS framework.

Disclosures are **non-optional** when applicable.

---

## Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosures exist to:
- Protect consumers
- Reduce transaction risk
- Ensure statutory compliance
- Prevent post-sale liability

TitleApp treats disclosures as **immutable compliance artifacts**.

---

## Core Nevada Disclosures (Baseline)

### Seller Disclosures
- Seller’s Real Property Disclosure (NRS 113)
- Known Defects Disclosure
- Environmental Hazards Disclosure (where applicable)

### Property Condition
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure (federal, if pre-1978)
- Flood Zone Disclosure
- HOA Disclosure Summary (if applicable)

### Transactional
- Agency Disclosure
- Dual Agency Disclosure (if applicable)

---

## Enforcement Rules

Disclosures:
- MUST be acknowledged before execution
- MAY NOT be edited or redlined
- MUST be retained with transaction records

Failure to deliver required disclosures may:
- Void agreements
- Trigger rescission rights
- Create civil liability

---

## Source of Authority

Primary:
- Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS)
- Nevada Real Estate Division (NRED)

Secondary:
- Federal consumer protection statutes

---

## Versioning & Updates

Each disclosure includes:
- Statutory reference
- Effective date
- Last-reviewed date

Updates are governed by the TitleApp RaaS compliance process.
