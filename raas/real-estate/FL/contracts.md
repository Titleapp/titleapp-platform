## Standard Contract Forms

### FAR/BAR (Florida Association of Realtors / Florida Bar)
The standard residential contract in Florida. Two main versions:

**FAR/BAR "As-Is" Contract:**
- Seller makes no repairs
- Buyer accepts property in current condition
- Buyer still has inspection period (default 15 days) to cancel for any reason
- Most common in FL market

**FAR/BAR Standard Contract:**
- Seller agrees to repair items up to a specified dollar amount
- Repair limit is negotiated (typically 1-1.5% of purchase price)
- Inspection period applies

### Key Contract Terms (Florida-specific)

**Effective Date:** The date the last party signs/initials the fully executed contract. All deadlines count from this date.

**Inspection Period (default 15 days):**
- Buyer can inspect for any reason
- Buyer can cancel for any reason during this period
- Deposit is fully refundable during inspection period
- This is the buyer's primary "out" in Florida
- AI tracks inspection deadline aggressively

**Financing Contingency:**
- Buyer must obtain loan commitment within specified days
- If denied: buyer can cancel, deposit refunded
- AI tracks loan commitment deadline

**Closing Date:**
- Specified in contract
- Extensions require written agreement from both parties
- AI alerts at 14 days, 7 days, 3 days before closing

**Escrow/Earnest Money:**
- Held by escrow agent (title company, brokerage, or attorney)
- Must be deposited within 3 business days of effective date (standard)
- AI tracks: deposit received? On time?

**Title Insurance:**
- In FL, who pays for title insurance varies by county
  - South FL (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach): buyer pays
  - Most of FL: seller pays (the "seller pays" counties)
  - Negotiable regardless of custom
- AI auto-applies county custom but flags for confirmation

**Survey:**
- Not required in FL but recommended
- Typically buyer's expense
