## Property Taxes

### Rates
- Texas has NO state income tax — property tax is the primary revenue source
- Total effective rate: typically 1.6-2.5%+ of assessed value
- Varies by county, city, school district, and special districts
- Example: a $400,000 home in Houston area may pay $8,000-$10,000+ annually
- AI MUST prominently display property tax estimate in every buyer cost analysis

### Homestead Exemption
- Primary residence: $100,000 exemption from school district taxes (as of 2023 legislation)
- Additional exemptions: over 65, disabled, veterans
- Tax freeze for over-65 and disabled homeowners (school district taxes frozen)
- Must file with county appraisal district
- AI reminds buyers to file for homestead exemption immediately after closing

### Protest
- Property owners can protest assessed values annually
- Deadline: May 15 or 30 days after notice, whichever is later
- Very common in TX — most protests result in some reduction
- AI can flag: "Your property assessment increased 15% this year. The protest deadline is May 15. Would you like guidance on protesting?"

## Transfer Taxes
- Texas has NO state or local transfer tax on real estate sales
- One of the few states with no transfer tax

## Title Insurance
- Rates are PROMULGATED by the Texas Department of Insurance
- Same rate from every title company — non-negotiable
- Based on property value
- Seller typically pays for owner's policy, buyer pays for lender's policy (negotiable)

## Insurance

### Homeowner's Insurance
- Required by lenders
- Texas rates are among the highest in the US (weather: hail, wind, hurricanes on coast, tornadoes)
- Average premium: $2,500-$4,500
- Hail damage is the #1 claim in most TX markets

### Wind/Hurricane Insurance
- Gulf Coast properties: wind coverage may be separate
- Texas Windstorm Insurance Association (TWIA) for coastal counties
- WPI-8 inspection required for TWIA eligibility
- AI flags coastal property insurance requirements

### Flood Insurance
- Required in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Areas
- Houston area: extremely flood-prone, many properties outside FEMA zones still flood
- AI flags: flood history, FEMA zone, and recommends flood insurance even outside mandatory zones in flood-prone areas
