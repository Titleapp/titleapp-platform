## 1. General Information

### 1.1 Purpose

This MEL identifies equipment that may be inoperative for dispatch under specific conditions, repair intervals, and operational/maintenance procedures. It is derived from the FAA Master Minimum Equipment List (MMEL) for the Pilatus PC-12/47E and has been customized for [Operator] Part 135 operations.

### 1.2 Repair Categories

| Category | Repair Interval | Description |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| A | As stated in Remarks | Specific timeframe per item |
| B | 3 consecutive calendar days | Excluding day of discovery |
| C | 10 consecutive calendar days | Excluding day of discovery |
| D | 120 consecutive calendar days | Excluding day of discovery |

### 1.3 Procedure Types

- **(M) Maintenance Procedure**: Must be performed by certificated maintenance personnel before dispatch.
- **(O) Operational Procedure**: May be performed by the flight crew before or during flight.
- If neither (M) nor (O) is specified, no special procedure is required beyond placarding.

### 1.4 Placarding

When an item is deferred under the MEL, it shall be placarded "INOPERATIVE" or with the specific placard required by the MEL item. Placards shall be in plain view of the pilot.

### 1.5 Column Definitions

- **Repair Category**: A, B, C, or D
- **Number Installed**: Total quantity installed on the aircraft
- **Number Required for Dispatch**: Minimum quantity required to be operative for dispatch
- **Remarks or Exceptions**: Conditions under which the item may be inoperative
- **(M) Procedures**: Maintenance actions required before dispatch
- **(O) Procedures**: Operational actions required by the flight crew

## 2. Non-Essential Furnishing (NEF) Deferral Program

Reference: FAA Order 8900.10

Items classified as Non-Essential Equipment and Furnishings may be deferred under the operator's NEF program. NEF items are tracked as Category D (120-day) deferrals. The NEF list is maintained separately. New NEF items must be vetted with the AMOC. Completed NEF items are reported to the FAA Certificate Management Team (CMT).
