# Aircraft De-Ice Equipment Training

> Part 135 Reference Template -- White-labeled for generic operator use.
> Source knowledge derived from initial/recurrent de-ice equipment training materials for fixed-wing operations (PC-12).
> Applicable season: October through April (adjust for local climate).

---

## 1. Overview

This document covers the two categories of de-icing equipment available at operator base locations:

1. **P40GPS Deicing Cart** -- A powered, heated fluid application system available at most fixed-wing bases (where permitted by airport authority). This is the primary de-icing tool.
2. **Portable De-Icing Equipment** -- Handheld spray bottles and wands with heated fluid for remote operations and light contamination. This equipment supports the **clean aircraft concept** but is **NOT an approved deicing program** and does not substitute for a formal FAA-approved deicing/anti-icing program.

Both systems use **Type 1 de-icing fluid** (most commonly DOW Type 1, premixed).

## 2. P40GPS Deicing Cart

### Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Tank | 40-gallon polyethylene |
| Engine | 5.0 HP Honda, 4-stroke |
| Fuel | 87 octane gasoline (unmixed -- do not use fuel/oil mix) |
| Oil | 10W-30 |
| Pump | 4-roller pump, 6 GPM, 120 PSI max |
| Fluid temperature | Max 160 F, self-regulated by thermostat |
| Spray nozzle | Gunjet 43 with 24-inch extension, adjustable pattern |
| Hose | 25 feet, 3/8-inch ID |
| Weight | 230 lbs empty / 630 lbs loaded |

Some base locations have **electric pump variants** instead of the Honda gas engine. Operating principles are the same; the power source differs.

### Seasonal Preparation Checklist

Before the winter season (and periodically throughout), complete the following:

1. **Replace fuel and check oil** -- Drain old gasoline, fill with fresh 87 octane unmixed. Check oil level (10W-30), top off or replace as needed.
2. **Function and leak check** -- Run the system using water or Type 1 fluid. Inspect all hoses, fittings, and connections for leaks. Check the pump for proper priming and pressure.
3. **Check valve function** -- Verify the in-line valve and pressure relief valve operate correctly. Confirm the spray nozzle opens, closes, and adjusts through its full range.
4. **Spray pressure and pattern** -- With the system running, verify the spray pressure is controllable from low to 120 PSI. Confirm the nozzle produces an even spray pattern at various settings.
5. **Recirculate** -- Run the system in recirculation mode to flush lines and verify flow path integrity.
6. **Heater operational check** -- With fluid in the tank, run the heater and verify fluid temperature reaches a minimum of **140 F**. The thermostat should regulate to a maximum of 160 F and cycle off. If the heater does not reach 140 F, take the unit out of service for repair.
7. **Fill with premixed Type 1 fluid** -- After all checks pass, drain the test fluid (if water was used) and fill the tank with premixed Type 1 de-icing fluid. The cart is now ready for the season.

### Operating Procedure

1. **Pre-operation checks:**
   - Verify fluid level in the tank. Refill with premixed Type 1 if low.
   - Verify fluid temperature. The tank should be plugged in to shore power whenever there is a **potential for icing conditions** -- the thermostat maintains fluid at 160 F.
   - If the fluid is cold, allow time for the heater to bring temperature to at least 140 F before spraying.

2. **Startup:**
   - Open the **in-line valve** (located between the tank and pump).
   - Set the **pressure reduction valve** to low/minimum for startup (prevents pressure spike on engine start).
   - Start the engine (Honda pull-start or electric start variant).
   - Allow the engine to warm briefly, then gradually increase the pressure reduction valve to the desired spray pressure.

3. **Application:**
   - Grip the spray nozzle wand and squeeze the trigger handle to spray.
   - Apply Type 1 fluid to contaminated surfaces: wings (top, bottom, leading edge), horizontal stabilizer, vertical stabilizer, control surfaces, windshield, and propeller as needed.
   - Work systematically from one end of the aircraft to the other. Ensure complete coverage of all critical surfaces.
   - Monitor fluid temperature and pressure during application.

4. **Shutdown:**
   - Release the trigger handle.
   - Turn off the engine.
   - **Relieve system pressure** -- Open the pressure reduction valve fully to bleed residual pressure from the hose and pump. This prevents pressure damage to seals and fittings during storage.
   - Close the in-line valve.
   - Plug the tank heater back into shore power to maintain fluid temperature.

## 3. Portable De-Icing Equipment -- Overview

Portable de-icing equipment is provided for:

- **Remote operations** where the P40GPS deicing cart is not available.
- **Light frost or thin ice removal** that does not require the full cart system.
- Maintaining the **clean aircraft concept** -- ensuring the aircraft departs with clean, uncontaminated critical surfaces.

> **IMPORTANT:** Portable de-icing equipment is intended primarily for **leading edge buildup and light frost**. It is **NOT an approved deicing program** under 14 CFR Part 135. Operators requiring a formal deicing/anti-icing program must use approved equipment and procedures per their Operations Specifications. Portable equipment supports, but does not replace, formal deicing.

**Type 1 fluid is most effective when heated.** Use the immersion heater (see Section 7) to heat fluid before application. Cold Type 1 fluid has significantly reduced de-icing effectiveness.

### Winter Operating Season

Portable de-icing equipment should be maintained, stocked, and available from **October through April** (adjust dates based on local climate and operator policy). Include portable de-icing equipment in the daily pre-flight check during winter months.

## 5. Spray Bottle and Wands

### Spray Bottle

- Capacity: 2-3 gallons of DOW Type 1 premixed de-icing fluid.
- Features a **quick-disconnect fitting** for attaching to either the short wand or extended wand. The quick-disconnect is similar to a compressed air fitting -- push to connect, pull the collar to disconnect. An internal pin prevents leaks when disconnected.
- Heat the fluid before loading the spray bottle (use the immersion heater). Cold fluid is less effective.
- Check the spray bottle fluid level during daily pre-flight in winter months. Refill as needed.

### Short Wand

- Best suited for: **Wings** (leading edge, upper surface, lower surface), **propeller**, and other surfaces within easy reach.
- Features an **adjustable nozzle** at the tip for spray pattern control (narrow stream to wide fan).
- Activation: **Lever or squeeze trigger** at the handle.
- Working technique: Hold the wand 6-12 inches from the surface. Apply Type 1 fluid in even passes across the contaminated area. Allow the fluid to break the bond between ice/frost and the aircraft surface, then wipe or allow the contamination to sheet off.

### Extended Wand

- Best suited for: **Tail surfaces** (horizontal stabilizer, vertical stabilizer, elevator, rudder) that cannot be reached from the ground with the short wand.
- Extends from approximately **4 feet to 12 feet**.
- Features an **adjustable nozzle** at the tip (same type as the short wand).
- Activation: **Turn-style on/off control** at the handle (twist to open, twist to close -- not a squeeze trigger).
- **Twist lock mechanism:** The telescoping sections are secured with a twist lock. Turn **counter-clockwise (CCW) to loosen** and extend; turn **clockwise (CW) to tighten** and lock at the desired length.

> **WARNING -- No stops to prevent over-extension.** The telescoping sections can be pulled completely apart if overextended. When fully extended, maintain approximately **6 inches of overlap** between the bottom section and the control knob section. Do not pull the wand apart.

- **Extended wand primary use:** Removing **leading edge ice from approach** (ice accumulated on tail leading edges during the approach and landing phase). If ice is suspected **aft of the leading edge** (on the upper or lower surface of the stabilizer, on control surfaces), a **visual inspection MUST follow** the de-icing application. The extended wand may not effectively remove contamination from aft surfaces at full extension, and crew cannot see the aft surfaces clearly from the ground.

### Nozzle Storage

- Store all nozzles in the **fully closed position** (clockwise / CW to close).
- Store nozzles **inverted in the orange bucket** when not in use to prevent contamination and damage.
- To operate: open the nozzle **1/4 to 1/2 turn counter-clockwise (CCW)** from fully closed. This provides a controlled spray pattern. Opening further increases flow rate but reduces spray precision.
- Closing the nozzle: Turn **clockwise (CW)** to increase pressure / narrow the stream, continuing CW to fully closed.

## 6. Quick-Disconnect Fitting

The quick-disconnect fitting connects the spray bottle to either wand:

- **Connection:** Push the wand fitting into the spray bottle outlet until it clicks into place. Tug gently to confirm it is locked.
- **Disconnection:** Pull the outer collar of the quick-disconnect back toward the spray bottle, then pull the wand fitting free.
- An internal **check pin** seals the spray bottle outlet when the wand is disconnected, preventing fluid leakage.
- The fitting is similar in design and operation to standard compressed air quick-disconnect fittings.

---

## 7. Immersion Heater

- **Purpose:** Heats Type 1 de-icing fluid in the orange 5-gallon bucket before transferring to the spray bottle. Warm fluid is significantly more effective at breaking ice bonds than cold fluid.
- **Two sizes available:** Narrow-neck and wide-neck variants to fit different bucket openings. Use the size that fits your bucket.
- **Operation:**
  1. Fill the orange bucket with Type 1 fluid.
  2. Insert the immersion heater into the fluid. Ensure the heating element is fully submerged -- **never operate the heater dry or partially submerged** (fire hazard and heater damage).
  3. Plug in the heater and **monitor during heating**. Do not leave the heater unattended.
  4. Heat the fluid until warm to the touch (approximately 120-140 F for effective de-icing). Do not boil.
  5. Remove the heater, transfer heated fluid to the spray bottle, and proceed with de-icing.

## 8. Fluid Application Guidelines

### Application Priority

Apply de-icing fluid to critical surfaces in the following priority order:

1. **Wings** -- Leading edges, upper surfaces, lower surfaces, and all control surfaces (ailerons, flaps).
2. **Horizontal stabilizer and elevator** -- Leading edges and control surfaces.
3. **Vertical stabilizer and rudder** -- Leading edge and control surface.
4. **Propeller** -- All blade surfaces.
5. **Windshield and windows** -- As needed for visibility.
6. **Pitot tubes, static ports, AOA vanes** -- Ensure all probes are clear of ice. Use care to avoid forcing fluid into ports.

### Clean Aircraft Concept

Before every departure during winter months, perform a **clean aircraft check**:

- All critical surfaces (wings, tail, control surfaces, propeller) must be free of frost, ice, snow, and any other contamination.
- If contamination is found, remove it using the available de-icing equipment before departure.
- If contamination cannot be fully removed with available equipment, **do not depart**. Coordinate with maintenance and/or request professional deicing services.
- The PIC is ultimately responsible for verifying the aircraft is clean before departure.

### Post-Application Inspection

After de-icing, perform a visual walk-around to verify:

- All ice, frost, and snow have been removed from critical surfaces.
- No residual ice remains in control surface gaps, hinge areas, or leading edge crevices.
- Control surfaces move freely through their full range (check by hand or during flight control check).
- Pitot covers and engine inlet plugs (if used) are removed.
