# Pilatus PC-12 -- Master Minimum Equipment List (MMEL) Reference

Document: EASA MMEL Document 02395, Issue 001, Revision 10
Issue Date: July 01, 2025
Aircraft: Pilatus PC-12/47E (all variants -- PC-12/45, NG, NGX)
MSN Applicability: 101-544, 545, 546-888, 1001-1719, 1720-2000, 2001-2219, 2221-3000, 3001+
Authority: EASA approved

## Rectification Interval Categories

| Category | Interval | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| A | As specified in remarks | Specific time/flight limit stated per item |
| B | 3 consecutive calendar days | Excluding day of discovery |
| C | 10 consecutive calendar days | Excluding day of discovery |
| D | 120 consecutive calendar days | Excluding day of discovery |

## Operation Categories

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| CAT | Commercial Air Transport |
| SPO | Specialized Operations |
| NCO | Non-Commercial Operations |
| ALL | All operation types |

## Item Based Relief

### ATA 21 -- Air Conditioning

**21-50-01 Temperature Control System**
- (A) Manual temperature control mode: Interval C. May be inoperative provided automatic temperature control is operative.
- (B) Automatic temperature control mode: Interval C. May be inoperative provided manual temperature control is operative and cabin temperature is maintained manually.

**21-60-01 Pressurization Control System**
- (A) Automatic pressurization control: Interval B. May be inoperative (CAT) provided manual pressurization control is operative, flight altitude limited to FL250, and crew monitors cabin altitude.
- (B) Manual pressurization control: Interval C. May be inoperative provided automatic pressurization control is operative.

**21-60-02 Cabin Pressure Safety Valve**
- Safety valve: No dispatch relief -- must be operative.

**21-60-03 Cabin Altitude Warning**
- Interval C. May be inoperative provided procedures do not require pressurized flight above FL250.

**21-60-04 Rate of Climb/Descent Indicator**
- Interval C. May be inoperative.

### ATA 22 -- Auto Flight

**22-10-01 Autopilot System**
- (A) (CAT): Interval B. May be inoperative provided yaw damper (if installed) is considered inoperative (refer to 22-10-01), and operations do not require autopilot use.
- (B) (NCO/SPO): Interval C. Same conditions as CAT.

**22-10-02 Yaw Damper System**
- (A) (SPO/NCO) (MSN 545, 1001-1719, 1721-1942): Interval C. May be inoperative provided autopilot is not used. Note: Above FL200, aircraft must be flown only in balanced flight (slip ball centered +/- 1 ball).
- (B) (CAT) (MSN 545, 1001-1719, 1721-1942): Interval B. Same conditions.
- (C) (SPO/NCO) (MSN 1720, 2001+): Interval C. May be inoperative provided autopilot is not used. Note: Above FL155, aircraft must be flown only in balanced flight (slip-skid indicator +/- 1 trapezoid) and above 140 KIAS (if practical). Use of Prop Low Speed function (if installed) with flaps retracted configuration is not permitted.
- (D) (CAT) (MSN 1720, 2001+): Interval B. Same conditions as (C).

**22-10-03 Flight Director System**
- (A) (CAT): Interval B. May be inoperative provided autopilot is considered inoperative (refer to 22-10-01).
- (B) (NCO/SPO): Interval C. Same conditions.

**22-10-04 Auto-throttle System**
- (MSN 1720, 2001+): Interval C. May be inoperative.

**22-10-05 Electronic Stability Protection (ESP)**
- (MSN 1720, 2001+): Interval C. May be inoperative provided autopilot is considered inoperative (refer to 22-10-01).

### ATA 23 -- Communications

**23-10-01 VHF Communication System**
- (A) COM 1: No dispatch relief. COM 1 must be operative.
- (B) COM 2 (CAT): Interval C. May be inoperative provided COM 2 is not required for any segment of the intended flight route, and datalink (if installed) is considered inoperative (refer to 23-20-01).
- (C) COM 2 (NCO/SPO): Interval D. Same conditions as (B).

**23-10-07 Audio System**
- (A) Audio 1: No dispatch relief.
- (B) Audio 2: Interval C. May be inoperative provided single pilot operations are not conducted.

**23-11-01 Cockpit Voice Recorder (CVR/CVFDR)**
- (A) (CAT): Interval B. May be inoperative provided repair or replacement is accomplished within 72 hours of discovery, and operations are not conducted in airspace where CVR is required.
- (B) (NCO/SPO): Interval D. May be inoperative.

**23-11-07 HF Communication System**
- Interval D. May be inoperative provided HF is not required for any segment of the intended flight route.

**23-20-01 Datalink Communication (CPDLC/ATC Datalink)**
- Interval D. May be inoperative provided procedures do not require datalink use.

**23-30-01 Emergency Locator Transmitter (ELT)**
- (A) (CAT): Interval B. May be inoperative provided repair or replacement is accomplished at the next point where one is available, and ELT is not required by the route.
- (B) (NCO/SPO): Interval C. Same conditions.

### ATA 24 -- Electrical Power

**24-30-01 Main Generator (Generator 1)**
- (A) (CAT): Interval C. May be inoperative provided non-essential loads are shed, flight duration limited, and second generator is operative.
- (B) (NCO/SPO): Interval C. Same conditions.

**24-30-02 Starter/Generator (Generator 2)**
- (A): Interval C. May be inoperative provided Generator 1 is operative, non-essential loads are shed, and external start capability is ensured at destination.

**24-30-03 Battery (MSN 3001+, Li-Ion)**
- Battery 1 or Battery 2: Interval C. May be inoperative provided remaining battery is operative and verified serviceable.

### ATA 28 -- Fuel

**28-20-01 Fuel Quantity Indicating System**
- (A) Fuel quantity indicators (both): No dispatch relief. At least one must be operative.
- (B) Low level sensing: Interval C. May be inoperative provided fuel quantity indicators are operative and fuel quantity is verified by other means (dipstick).

**28-40-01 Fuel Crossfeed**
- Interval B. May be inoperative provided flight time is limited to fuel quantity in selected tank.

### ATA 30 -- Ice and Rain Protection

**30-10-01 Engine Anti-Ice**
- No dispatch relief. Must be operative when flight in icing conditions is anticipated.

**30-20-01 Airframe De-Ice (Pneumatic Boots)**
- (A) (FIKI): No dispatch relief. Must be operative for flight into known icing.
- (B) (Non-FIKI): May be inoperative provided flight is not conducted in known icing conditions.

**30-20-02 Windshield Anti-Ice/De-Ice**
- No dispatch relief for flight into known icing. Must be operative.

**30-30-01 Pitot/Static Heat**
- No dispatch relief. All pitot and static heating must be operative.

**30-40-01 Propeller De-Ice**
- (A) (FIKI): No dispatch relief. Must be operative for flight into known icing.
- (B) (Non-FIKI): Interval C. May be inoperative provided flight is not conducted in known icing conditions.

### ATA 31 -- Indicating / Recording Systems

**31-10-01 Flight Data Recorder (FDR/CVFDR)**
- (A) (CAT): Interval B. May be inoperative provided repair is accomplished within 72 hours, and FDR is not required by operations spec.
- (B) (NCO/SPO): Interval D. May be inoperative.

**31-20-01 Clock**

**31-60-01 CAS (Crew Alerting System) Display**

**33-10-01 Flight Compartment Lighting**
- (A) Instrument panel lighting: No dispatch relief for night operations.
- (B) Dome/utility lights: Interval C for night operations, provided instrument lighting is operative.

**33-40-01 Exterior Lighting -- Landing/Taxi Lights**
- (A) Landing lights (night): No dispatch relief. At least one landing light must be operative for night operations.
- (B) Landing lights (day): Interval D. May be inoperative.
- (C) Taxi light: Interval D. May be inoperative.

**33-40-02 Exterior Lighting -- Navigation Lights**
- No dispatch relief. All navigation lights must be operative for night operations.

**33-40-03 Exterior Lighting -- Anti-Collision / Strobe Lights**
- (A) Anti-collision (beacon): Interval C. May be inoperative provided strobe lights are operative.
- (B) Strobe lights: Interval C. May be inoperative provided anti-collision beacon is operative.

**33-40-04 Exterior Lighting -- Ice Inspection Light**
- (A) (FIKI): No dispatch relief. Must be operative for night flight into known icing.
- (B) (Non-FIKI night): Interval C. May be inoperative.

- Interval C. May be inoperative.

**34-20-03 Standby Attitude Indication**

**34-40-01 TCAS (Traffic Alert and Collision Avoidance System)**

**34-41-01 Weather Detection Systems**

- 34-41-01-2 Lightning Sensor / Stormscope: Interval D. May be inoperative provided stormscope is deactivated and procedures do not require stormscope use.

**34-43-01 TAWS Class B**

**34-43-02 TAWS Class A**

**34-49-01 (S)XM Data Receiver**

**34-50-01 Flight Management System (FMS)**

- (B): Interval C. Same conditions for NCO/SPO.

**34-50-01-1 Navigation Databases**

**34-51-01 Navigation Systems (VOR, DME, ADF, GNSS, INS, RMI)**

## High-Criticality Items (No Dispatch Relief)

The following systems have NO dispatch relief in the MMEL and must always be operative:

- Primary flight controls (ATA 27-00-01)
- Stick pusher / stick shaker (ATA 27-50-01)
- Engine fire detection (ATA 26-11-01)
- Engine fire extinguishing (ATA 26-12-01)
- Flight envelope protection warning (overspeed, stall) (ATA 34-10-02)
- CAS display (ATA 31-60-01)
- Landing gear retraction system (ATA 32-10-01)
- Landing gear green (down/locked) indication (ATA 32-30-01A)
- Normal braking (ATA 32-41-01)
- Cabin emergency lighting (ATA 25-60-01)
- Navigation lights (night) (ATA 33-40-02)
- VHF COM 1 (ATA 23-10-01A)
- Audio 1 (ATA 23-10-07A)
- Pitot/static heat (ATA 30-30-01)
- Engine anti-ice (when icing anticipated) (ATA 30-10-01)
- Windshield anti-ice (when icing anticipated) (ATA 30-20-02)
- Airframe de-ice (FIKI operations) (ATA 30-20-01A)
- Propeller de-ice (FIKI operations) (ATA 30-40-01A)
- Flight crew oxygen (pressurized flight above FL250) (ATA 35-10-01A)
- Passenger oxygen (above FL250 with passengers) (ATA 35-20-01A)
- Cabin door/cargo door/emergency exit seals (pressurized flight) (ATA 52-20-01/02/03)
- At least one fuel quantity indicator (ATA 28-20-01A)
- Instrument panel lighting (night) (ATA 33-10-01A)
- At least one landing light (night) (ATA 33-40-01A)
