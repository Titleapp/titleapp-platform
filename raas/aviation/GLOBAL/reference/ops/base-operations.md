### 4.2 Fuel Inspection

Daily fuel system inspections are required per company Fuel Handling Procedures:
- Sump samples from all drain points
- Water detection test
- Clear and bright verification
- Filter differential pressure check (if applicable)
- Record results in the fuel inspection log

### 4.3 FBO Fuel (Ad-Hoc)

When using fuel from an FBO or other non-company source:
- Request daily fuel inspection documentation
- If unavailable, perform water detection test and clear-and-bright test using onboard equipment
- Do NOT dispense fuel of questionable quality
- Record ad-hoc fuel source in the fuel usage log

### 11.3 AOG (Aircraft on Ground) Procedures

If the aircraft goes AOG at base:
1. Notify OCC and MMOC immediately
2. Document the discrepancy in the logbook and electronic system
3. If company mechanic is not available, contact MMOC for contract maintenance authorization
4. Coordinate with ACP for operational coverage (backup aircraft, mutual aid)
5. Maintain communication with OCC on expected return-to-service timeline
