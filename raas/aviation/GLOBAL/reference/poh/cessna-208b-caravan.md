## Engine Indication System (EIS)

The G1000 EIS displays on two pages (ENGINE and SYSTEM):

### ENGINE Page Parameters
- Engine torque (TRQ) -- ft-lbs x 100
- Interstage Turbine Temperature (ITT) -- deg C
- Gas generator RPM (Ng) -- percent
- Propeller RPM
- Oil pressure (PSI)
- Oil temperature (deg C)
- Fuel quantity (lbs)
- Fuel flow (PPH)
- Battery amps
- Bus voltage
- Anti-ice fluid remaining (gallons)

### Torque Gauge
- Green arc: 0 to dynamic redline
- Dynamic redline: Adjusts automatically for OAT, altitude, inertial separator position, and bleed air heat position
- Below 16,000 ft: Represents takeoff power
- Above 16,000 ft: Represents maximum rated power
- Blue torque bug (1,600--1,900 RPM): Maximum allowed cruise torque

### ITT Gauge (Grand Caravan EX)
- Green band: 100--825 deg C (engine running)
- Amber caution band: 826--849 deg C
- Red line: 850 deg C

### ITT Gauge (675 SHP Caravans)
- Green band: 100--740 deg C (engine running)
- Amber caution: 765--805 deg C
- Red line: 805 deg C

### Ng Gauge
- Redline: 103.7% (EX) / 101.6% (675 SHP)

### Prop RPM
- White: 0--1,599
- Green: 1,600--1,900
- Red background: Above 1,901

## Systems Summary

### Electrical
- 28-volt DC system
- 24-volt NiCad battery
- Starter-generator: 200 amp (675 SHP) or 300 amp (EX)
- Standby alternator: 75 amp, belt-driven
- Dual bus architecture

### Fuel System
- Two wing tanks with gravity feed to fuselage reservoir
- Ejector boost pump (engine-driven motive flow) + electric auxiliary boost pump
- Oil-to-fuel heater
- Engine-driven fuel pump
- Fuel control unit with Ng governor
- Flow divider to 14 simplex nozzles (10 primary + 4 secondary)
- Fuel shutoff valve (firewall mounted, red push-pull knob)
- Fuel selectors: LEFT ON/OFF and RIGHT ON/OFF (overhead panel)
- Normal operation: Both tanks ON

### Air Induction
- Ram air inlet through inertial separator system
- Inertial separator: NORMAL (push) for standard operations, BYPASS (pull) for visible moisture, low temps (below 41 deg F / 5 deg C), dust, or sandy conditions
- Compressor inlet screen prevents large particles

### Propeller System
- Governor operates in 5 modes: Underspeed, Onspeed, Overspeed, Feathering, Beta range
- BETA range: Non-governing, pilot controls blade pitch from idle thrust through zero to max reverse
- Feathering: Counterweights and spring tension drive blades to feathered position when oil pressure drops
- Overspeed governor: Tested during runup, resets at 1,750 +/- 60 RPM

### Ice Protection
- TKS anti-ice system (Grand Caravan EX) or pneumatic deicing boots (675 SHP)
- Prop anti-ice: Electrical
- Windshield anti-ice: Electrical panel
- Inertial separator BYPASS for visible moisture

### Engine Fire Detection
- Heat sensor in engine compartment
- Red ENGINE FIRE CAS message
- Warning horn above pilot
- FIRE DETECT test switch on instrument panel
- Cabin heat firewall shutoff knob (push-pull, shuts off bleed air supply and return lines)

### Engine Trend Monitoring
- ALTAIR Avionics ADASd system monitors engine parameters
- Stores 30,000+ engine runs/events
- Interfaces with P&W Turbine Tracker website for TBO extension analysis
- Monitors ITT, fuel flow, Ng, torque, prop RPM, oil pressure
- CAS messages: ETM EXCEED (amber/white), PREV EXCEED, ETM CAPTURE, ETM FAULT

## Operating Limitations

### Engine Starting Precautions
- Hot start: Caused by excessive fuel flow at normal RPM or normal fuel flow with insufficient RPM. If Ng accelerates rapidly above 20%, suspect gear train decouple. If no ITT rise within 10 seconds after moving FUEL CONDITION to LOW IDLE, or ITT rapidly approaches 1,090 deg C, move FUEL CONDITION to CUTOFF.
- After aborted start: Allow adequate time to drain unburned fuel. Dry motoring within starter limitations after confirming fuel drainage.
- Cold weather: APU recommended below 0 deg F (-18 deg C). GPU/EPU must be negatively grounded, regulated to 28 VDC, minimum 800 amps during start cycle. Do not use GPU/EPU exceeding 1,700 amps.

### General Limitations
- Maximum operating altitude: 25,000 ft
- Maximum rudder sideslip duration: 3 minutes (due to fuel starvation risk)
- Fuel must contain anti-icing additive per MIL-I-27686 (EGME) or MIL-I-85470 (DIEGME)
- Do not move POWER lever aft of IDLE when propeller is feathered or engine is not running
