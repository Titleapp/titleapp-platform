## 6. Engine Data -- Continental IO-550-C

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Engine Model | Teledyne Continental IO-550-C |
| Rated Power | 300 HP each |
| TBO | 1,700 hours |
| Cylinders | 6, horizontally opposed |
| Fuel System | Fuel-injected (position-tuned) |
| Ignition | Dual magneto |
| Lubrication | Wet-sump, pressure-type |
| Oil Capacity | 12 quarts per engine |
| Oil Temperature -- Max | 116 C |
| Oil Temperature -- Cruise (normal) | 70-82 C |
| Oil Temperature -- Min for operation above 1,200 RPM | 24 C |
| Cylinder Head Temp -- Max | 238 C |
| Cylinder Head Temp -- Normal cruise | 150-200 C |
| Magneto RPM Drop -- Max | 150 RPM per magneto |
| Magneto Differential -- Max | 50 RPM between magnetos on same engine |
| Governor Range | 2,000-2,700 RPM |
| Feather Lock Pin Engagement | Below 600-700 RPM |

### Mixture Management
- Full rich for takeoff and climb (adjust per cyan pointer on fuel flow gauge)
- Lean to peak EGT or lean of peak for cruise
- Operation at settings leaner than 27 C lean of peak EGT is PROHIBITED
- Use LEAN ASSIST function on G1000 MFD for proper leaning

### Starter Limits
- Max 30 seconds cranking in a 4-minute period
- Do not exceed 1,200 RPM during start

### Engine Operating Precautions
- Preheat engines if ambient temperature below 20 F
- Do not exceed 1,200 RPM until oil temperature above 24 C
- Avoid manifold pressure and RPM combinations in the non-operating area of the MP vs RPM chart
- Max power reduction rate in descent: 2 inches MAP every 2 minutes
- Open cowl flaps for ground operations and climb; close for cruise and descent

## 7. Systems Summary

### Electrical
| Component | Specification |
|---|---|
| System Voltage | 28 VDC |
| Alternators | Two 100-ampere, 28.5V alternators |
| Batteries | Two 24V batteries |

### Avionics
| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Flight Deck | Garmin G1000 integrated |
| PFD | Primary Flight Display (pilot and copilot) |
| MFD | Multifunction Display (center) |
| EIS | Engine Indication System (left side of MFD) |
| GEA 71 | Engine/airframe interface unit |
| Autopilot | GFC 700 AFCS |
| Alerting | G1000 CAS: Red = Warning, Yellow = Caution, White = Advisory |

### Fuel System
- Gravity-fed from wing tanks
- Three rubber bladder cells + one 14-gal wet wing tip cell per wing
- Engine-driven fuel pump (positive displacement, sliding vane)
- Electric boost pumps (HI / LO / OFF) per engine
- Crossfeed lines for emergency use (level flight only)
- Fuel flow divider distributes to six injector nozzles per engine
- Manual mixture control
- Position-tuned fuel injection (fuel delivery proportional to air intake)

### Propellers
- Hartzell 3-blade, constant-speed, full-feathering
- Governor range: 2,000-2,700 RPM
- Feather lock pins engage below 600-700 RPM (prevent uncommanded feathering on ground)
- Unfeathering accumulators (nitrogen-charged, 100 psi)
- Propeller synchronizer (PROP SYNC switch, range approx 25 RPM)
- Propeller synchroscope (pointer on tachometer, indicates slower engine)
- Check feathering at 1,500 RPM during runup (max 300 RPM drop)
- Check governor at 2,200 RPM (stabilize at 2,000 RPM at detent)

### Lighting
- Landing lights: nacelle-mounted
- Taxi light: nosewheel-mounted
- Wing ice light: left nacelle
- Recognition, navigation, strobe, beacon lights standard
- Cabin, instrument, flood lighting

### Master Warning System
- Garmin G1000 Crew Alerting System (CAS)
- Warnings: Red -- immediate action required
- Cautions: Yellow -- awareness and possible action
- Advisories: White -- informational

## 10. Single-Engine Procedures

### Critical Speeds (OEI)
| Speed | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Vmca | 83 KIAS | Min control speed, air |

Note: Vyse and Vxse for the G58 are in the POH Section 2 / Emergency Procedures. The PTM pages reviewed do not list these values explicitly. Refer to the approved AFM.

### Engine Failure After Takeoff (Memory Items)
1. Mixtures -- FULL RICH (both)
2. Props -- FULL FORWARD (both)
3. Throttles -- FULL POWER (both)
4. Flaps -- UP
5. Landing gear -- UP (verify positive rate of climb)
6. Identify -- dead foot = dead engine
7. Verify -- retard throttle on suspected failed engine, observe instruments
8. Failed engine -- SECURE (mixture CUTOFF, prop FEATHER, magnetos OFF, fuel selector OFF)
9. Maintain Vyse or blue line speed

### Engine Securing Procedure
1. Throttle -- IDLE
2. Mixture -- IDLE CUTOFF
3. Prop -- FEATHER
4. Magnetos -- OFF
5. Fuel selector -- OFF
6. Alternator -- OFF
7. Cowl flap -- CLOSED

### Propeller Feathering Notes
- Move propeller control lever aft past the mechanical detent into feather
- Feather lock pins prevent feathering below 600-700 RPM
- If engine fails and RPM is decreasing, attempt to feather promptly before RPM drops below 700
- Unfeathering accumulators (if equipped) allow restart by moving prop lever out of feather detent
- Nitrogen charge pushes stored oil into prop dome, causing windmill for combustion

### Airstart Procedure (from feather)
1. Magnetos -- BOTH
2. Mixture -- FULL RICH
3. Fuel selector -- ON (appropriate tank)
4. Boost pump -- HI
5. Prop lever -- out of feather (unfeathering accumulator provides windmill)
6. If prop windmills above 700 RPM, engine should start
7. If prop does not windmill above 700 RPM, feather lock pins prevent rotation -- starter may be needed
8. Once running, advance throttle and reduce to cruise power
9. Boost pump -- LO or OFF as appropriate

### Notes
- Maintain blue line (Vyse) until at safe altitude and obstacles cleared
- Bank 3-5 degrees toward operating engine
- Close cowl flap on failed engine to reduce drag
- Do not attempt restart below safe altitude unless cause is identified
- Crossfeed available for emergency fuel management (level flight only)
- Refer to POH Emergency Procedures for complete checklists
