## Systems Overview

### Electrical
- Dual alternators (28V system)
- Dual batteries (Battery 1, Battery 2)
- Essential bus, Main bus 1, Main bus 2
- Avionics power switch

### Fuel
- Two wing tanks (left/right, 46 gal usable each)
- Fuel selector: LEFT / RIGHT
- Electric fuel pump: PRIME / BOOST / OFF
- Engine-driven fuel pump (primary)
- Low fuel quantity annunciation: 14 gal per side threshold
- Fuel imbalance annunciation: 10 gal threshold

### Flight Controls
- Side yoke (control stick)
- Rudder pedals with toe brakes
- Electric pitch trim, roll trim
- Flap control: UP (0%) / 50% / 100%

### CAPS (Cirrus Airframe Parachute System)
- Whole-aircraft ballistic recovery parachute
- Max deployment speed (Vpd): 140 KIAS
- Activation: Pull T-handle firmly on cabin ceiling
- Impact equivalent: approximately 13-foot drop
- Deployment procedure: Level wings, power idle, mixture cutoff, fuel off, seat belts tight, then pull handle
- Used for: loss of control, structural failure, midair collision, pilot incapacitation, unrecoverable spin entry, engine failure with no suitable landing site

## Disclaimer

This reference file is a structured summary of data extracted from the Cirrus SR22 Pilot's Operating Handbook (P/N 13772-006, Original Issue, December 28, 2016, applicable to serial numbers SR22-4435 and subsequent with Cirrus Perspective+ avionics). It is intended as a quick-reference resource for Digital Workers and does not replace the official POH/AFM. Pilots must always refer to the FAA-approved Airplane Flight Manual specific to their aircraft serial number for regulatory compliance and operational decisions. Performance data is approximate and based on standard conditions unless noted. Actual performance will vary with aircraft condition, configuration, weight, altitude, temperature, and pilot technique. Aircraft with optional Air Conditioning System will experience reduced climb performance (~50 fpm) and reduced cruise performance (~2 knots / ~6 BHP). Aircraft with optional Enhanced Vision System will experience approximately 0.5% range reduction and 1 knot cruise speed reduction.
