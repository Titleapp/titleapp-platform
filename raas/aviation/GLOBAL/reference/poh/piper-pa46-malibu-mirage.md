## 8. Systems Summary

### Electrical
- 2x engine-driven alternators
- Battery Master switch
- Dual alternator failure procedure available

### Landing Gear
- Hydraulically actuated retractable tricycle gear
- Emergency extension procedure (manual pump / gravity)
- Gear down airspeed approximately 70% of gear-retracted airspeed at same power

### Pressurization
- Maximum cabin differential pressure: 5.5 PSI
- Maximum operating altitude: 25,000 ft
- At 25,000 ft with 5.5 PSI differential, cabin altitude approximately 8,000 ft
- Below 12,500 ft aircraft altitude: leave cabin altitude control at takeoff setting
- Above 12,500 ft: set cabin altitude 500 ft above cruise altitude for smoother operation
- Cabin rate of climb: approximately 500 fpm at 9 o'clock position on rate control
- WARNING: Do not land with aircraft pressurized
- Pressurization controls: cabin altitude controller with rate of change, differential pressure/rate of climb gauge, dump/normal switch, pressurization control

### Propeller
- 3-blade constant speed, 80" diameter
- Full throttle: 2500 RPM
- Propeller governor check per normal procedures

### Fuel System
- 2 wing tanks (61 gal each, 60 usable)
- Engine-driven fuel pump + electric boost
- Fuel selectors: ON for each tank

### De-Ice / Anti-Ice
- Pneumatic boots (wing and tail leading edges)
- Max boot inflation speed: 178 KIAS
- Propeller and windshield anti-ice available
- Known icing equipment installed (with ice protection system per Section 5.2)
- Severe icing conditions: exit immediately, request priority handling from ATC
- Do not extend flaps when holding in icing conditions
- Do not engage autopilot in icing conditions

## 10. Operating Limitations

- Maximum operating altitude: 25,000 ft.
- Maximum cabin pressure differential: 5.5 PSI.
- Do not land with the aircraft pressurized.
- Severe icing: exit immediately if encountered (procedures in Section 4.50).
- Acrobatic maneuvers (including spins) are prohibited.
- Do not exceed Vne (198 KIAS) or Vno (168 KIAS) in turbulence.
- In turbulent air, reduce to Va (133 KIAS at max weight, proportionally less at lighter weights).
- Noise level: 74.7 dB(A) with 2-blade propeller, 79.7 dB(A) with 3-blade propeller (FAR 36).

## 11. Pressurization Operating Notes

### Normal Operation
- Set cabin altitude (outer scale) to 500 ft above field elevation before takeoff.
- Pressurization begins automatically as cabin passes through selected altitude.
- Below 12,500 ft aircraft altitude: leave at takeoff setting.
- Above 12,500 ft: reset aircraft altitude (inner scale) to 500 ft above cruise altitude.
- Adjust cabin rate of climb as desired. Normal letdown: 9 o'clock position (~500 fpm cabin rate).
- For descent: set cabin altitude to 500 ft above landing field elevation before starting letdown.
- Rate of control knob: increase for rapid descents so aircraft altitude does not outrun cabin.

### Cabin Altitude at Various Aircraft Altitudes (5.5 PSI Max Differential)

| Aircraft Altitude (ft) | Approximate Cabin Altitude (ft) |
|---|---|
| 12,500 | Sea level to field elevation |
| 15,000 | ~2,500 |
| 20,000 | ~5,500 |
| 25,000 | ~8,000 |

### Preflight Pressurization Check
- Check that cabin pressurization control lever moves with firm effort.
- If little effort required, suspect broken cable -- air control valve may have failed open or closed.
- Failed open: pressurized flight not possible, unpressurized flight OK.
- Failed closed: pressurized flight possible, but do not attempt (no fresh air exchange = contamination risk).
