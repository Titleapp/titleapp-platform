## Normal Checklist (Abbreviated)

### Preflight Inspection

1. Documents -- ARROW check (Airworthiness, Registration, Radio license, Operating handbook, Weight & Balance)
2. Fuel quantity -- check visually, caps secure
3. Oil -- check (6--8 qt)
4. Walkaround -- complete (control surfaces, tires, pitot tube, antennas, cowl, exhaust, prop)
5. Fuel selector -- BOTH
6. Master switch -- OFF
7. Mixture -- idle cutoff
8. Throttle -- 1/4 in open
9. Propeller -- full INCREASE (high RPM)
10. Trim -- takeoff position

### Engine Start

1. Brakes -- set (parking brake)
2. Mixture -- RICH
3. Propeller -- full INCREASE
4. Throttle -- 1/4 in open
5. Master switch -- ON (BOTH)
6. Beacon -- ON
7. Fuel pump -- ON (3--5 seconds, or until fuel pressure stabilizes)
8. Fuel pump -- OFF
9. Ignition -- START (release at catch)
10. Oil pressure -- green within 30 seconds
11. Throttle -- 1000 RPM (warm-up)
12. Avionics master -- ON

### Before Takeoff (Run-Up)

1. Brakes -- hold
2. Throttle -- 1800 RPM
3. Magnetos -- check L, R, BOTH (max drop 150 RPM, max diff 50 RPM)
4. Propeller -- cycle (full decrease, then full increase), 2--3 times
5. Carburetor heat -- N/A (fuel injected)
6. Engine instruments -- green
7. Suction -- 4.8--5.2 in Hg
8. Throttle -- idle, check idle speed
9. Throttle -- 1000 RPM
10. Flight instruments -- set
11. Trim -- takeoff
12. Fuel selector -- BOTH
13. Mixture -- RICH (or leaned for field elevation above 3000 ft)
14. Flaps -- set as desired
15. Doors and windows -- closed and locked
16. Seatbelts -- secure

### Normal Takeoff

1. Flaps -- 0 deg (or 20 deg for soft field)
2. Throttle -- full open smoothly
3. Prop -- full INCREASE (already set)
4. Mixture -- RICH
5. Engine instruments -- check
6. At 55 KIAS -- rotate
7. Climb at Vy (80 KIAS)
8. Above 500 ft AGL -- reduce to 25" MP / 2400 RPM (or as desired)

### Short Field Takeoff

1. Flaps -- 20 deg
2. Brakes -- hold, full power, check instruments
3. Brakes -- release
4. At 50 KIAS -- rotate
5. Climb at Vx (63 KIAS) until obstacle cleared
6. Flaps -- retract incrementally above 60 KIAS
7. Accelerate to Vy (80 KIAS)

### Cruise

1. Altitude -- stabilize
2. Power -- set (typically 23" MP / 2300 RPM for 65% power)
3. Mixture -- lean for best economy or best power
4. Trim -- adjust
5. Fuel selector -- BOTH
6. Engine instruments -- monitor

### Approach and Landing

1. ATIS -- obtain
2. Fuel selector -- BOTH
3. Mixture -- RICH
4. Propeller -- full INCREASE (2400 RPM)
5. Flaps -- as desired (10 deg initially)
6. Airspeed -- 75 KIAS on base
7. Flaps -- full on final
8. Airspeed -- 65 KIAS on final (60 KIAS short field)
9. Trim -- adjust
10. Touchdown -- main gear first, hold nosewheel off

### Short Field Landing

1. Full flaps
2. Airspeed -- 60 KIAS over obstacle
3. Power -- as needed for glidepath
4. Touchdown -- minimum speed, main gear first
5. Brakes -- maximum, flaps UP (dump lift)
6. Mixture -- idle cutoff if stopping

### After Landing

1. Flaps -- UP
2. Carburetor heat -- OFF (N/A, fuel injected)
3. Trim -- takeoff position
4. Transponder -- STBY

### Engine Shutdown

1. Avionics master -- OFF
2. Throttle -- 1000 RPM
3. Mixture -- idle cutoff (lean to stop)
4. Ignition -- OFF (after engine stops)
5. Master switch -- OFF
6. Fuel selector -- LEFT (or OFF)
7. Control lock -- install

## Emergency Checklist

### Engine Failure on Takeoff (low altitude)

1. Airspeed -- 65 KIAS (best glide)
2. Mixture -- idle cutoff
3. Fuel selector -- OFF
4. Ignition -- OFF
5. Master switch -- OFF (if not needed for flaps)
6. Flaps -- as required
7. Land straight ahead (do NOT attempt return to runway below 1000 ft AGL)

### Engine Failure in Flight

1. Airspeed -- 65 KIAS (best glide)
2. Fuel selector -- BOTH (or switch tanks)
3. Mixture -- RICH
4. Fuel pump -- ON
5. Ignition -- check BOTH
6. Throttle -- advance
7. If no restart, execute forced landing

### Engine Fire on Ground

1. Mixture -- idle cutoff
2. Throttle -- full open (draw fire into engine)
3. Fuel selector -- OFF
4. Master switch -- OFF
5. Evacuate
6. Extinguisher -- use if available

### Engine Fire in Flight

1. Mixture -- idle cutoff
2. Fuel selector -- OFF
3. Master switch -- OFF
4. Cabin heat -- OFF
5. Airspeed -- 100 KIAS (sideslip to keep flames away)
6. Execute emergency landing

### Electrical Fire in Flight

1. Master switch -- OFF
2. Avionics master -- OFF
3. All electrical switches -- OFF
4. Vents/cabin air -- open
5. Extinguisher -- use on visible fire
6. If fire appears out, master ON, check circuits one at a time
7. Land as soon as practical

### Loss of Oil Pressure

1. Land as soon as possible
2. Select nearest field
3. Power -- reduce to minimum needed
4. Prepare for engine seizure / forced landing

### Spin Recovery (PARE)

1. Power -- idle
2. Ailerons -- neutral
3. Rudder -- full opposite to spin direction
4. Elevator -- briskly forward through neutral
5. Rudder -- neutral when rotation stops
6. Wings -- level, recover from dive

### Vacuum System Failure

1. Confirm failure (suction gauge zero)
2. Attitude gyro and directional gyro -- unreliable
3. Use turn coordinator, magnetic compass, GPS for navigation
4. Advise ATC, request vectors if IMC

## Systems Summary

### Engine (IO-540)
6-cylinder, fuel-injected, horizontally opposed, air-cooled. 230 hp at 2400 RPM. Controlled by throttle (manifold pressure), propeller (RPM), and mixture. No carburetor heat (fuel-injected).

### Fuel System
Two integral wing tanks (46 gal each, 43.5 usable each). Fuel selector has 4 positions: LEFT, BOTH, RIGHT, OFF. Normal operation on BOTH. Engine-driven fuel pump with electric auxiliary pump for starting and emergencies. Fuel injection system.

### Electrical System
28V DC, engine-driven 60-amp alternator + 24V battery. Master switch has two halves (ALT and BAT). Avionics on dedicated avionics master bus. Voltage regulator protects system.

### Propeller
McCauley 2-blade constant speed. Prop lever controls governor: full forward = high RPM (fine pitch), full aft = low RPM (coarse pitch). Oil pressure drives to fine pitch; counterweights/spring drive to coarse.

### Flight Controls
Conventional yoke and rudder pedals. Manual flaps (Johnson bar) on 182S; electric flaps on 182T. Electric pitch trim. Rudder/aileron interconnect spring. No roll trim.

### Landing Gear
Fixed tricycle gear. Nose wheel steerable via rudder pedals. Hydraulic disc brakes on main gear. Fixed gear simplifies operations and maintenance.
