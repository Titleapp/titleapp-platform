## Normal Checklist (Abbreviated)

### Pre-Flight Inspection

**Cabin Check:**
1. MET, NAV, Mass & CG -- flight planning completed
2. Airplane documents -- complete and up-to-date
3. ELECTRIC MASTER -- OFF, pull out key
4. ENGINE MASTER -- check OFF
5. ECU SWAP -- check AUTOMATIC
6. Emergency fuel valve -- locked in NORMAL position
7. Front canopy & rear door -- clean, undamaged, locking OK
8. All electrical equipment -- OFF
9. Circuit breakers -- set in (check if any pulled)
10. Power lever -- check condition, freedom, full travel
11. Power lever -- IDLE
12. ELECTRIC MASTER -- ON
13. Fuel quantity -- check, use alternate mean
14. Lights (position, strobe) -- check
15. ELECTRIC MASTER -- OFF
16. Foreign objects -- check
17. Controls and trim -- free and correct
18. Baggage -- stowed and secured

### Before Starting Engine

1. Safety harnesses -- fastened
2. Brakes -- set (parking brake or toe brakes)
3. ELECTRIC MASTER -- ON
4. ENGINE MASTER -- ON
5. Wait for GLOW light to extinguish
6. Power lever -- IDLE
7. ELECTRIC MASTER -- START (release when engine running)
8. Oil pressure -- check (green range within 10 seconds)
9. Engine instruments -- monitor (all green)

### Before Takeoff

1. Brakes -- hold
2. Engine instruments -- all green
3. Power lever -- check response
4. ECU SWAP -- check ECU B, then back to AUTOMATIC
5. Trim -- set for takeoff
6. Flaps -- T/O position
7. Fuel quantity -- sufficient
8. Controls -- free and correct
9. Doors -- closed and locked (DOOR light OFF)
10. Safety harnesses -- secure
11. Transponder -- set

### Takeoff

1. Power lever -- MAX
2. Engine instruments -- check (all green)
3. Brakes -- release
4. Airspeed -- alive
5. At Vr (59 KIAS at 1150 kg) -- rotate
6. Climb at Vy (66 KIAS at 1150 kg), Flaps T/O
7. Above safe altitude -- Flaps UP
8. Cruise climb -- 73 KIAS (at 1150 kg)

### Cruise

1. Power -- set as required
2. Engine instruments -- all green
3. Fuel quantity -- monitor
4. Fuel transfer -- as required (LR tank: transfer AUX to MAIN)

### Landing Approach

1. Fuel quantity -- check
2. Safety harnesses -- secure
3. Power -- as required
4. Flaps -- T/O, then LDG when runway assured
5. Airspeed -- 71 KIAS (at 1150 kg), 63 KIAS (at 1000 kg)
6. Trim -- adjust

### After Landing

1. Flaps -- UP
2. Power lever -- IDLE
3. Lights -- as required

### Engine Shutdown

1. Power lever -- IDLE (allow 2-minute cool-down)
2. AVIONIC MASTER -- OFF
3. ENGINE MASTER -- OFF
4. ELECTRIC MASTER -- OFF

## Emergency Checklist

### Engine Failure After Takeoff (cannot abort)

1. Airspeed -- 72 KIAS (1150 kg) / 66 KIAS (1000 kg) / 59 KIAS (850 kg)
2. Power lever -- check MAX
3. ENGINE MASTER -- check ON
4. ECU SWAP -- ECU B
5. If no improvement -- emergency landing straight ahead

### Engine Problems in Flight -- Loss of Power

1. Airspeed -- 73 KIAS (1150 kg) / 68 KIAS (1000 kg) / 60 KIAS (850 kg)
2. Power lever -- MAX
3. If in icing -- Alternate Air ON
4. Fuel qty MAIN tank -- check
5. Fuel transfer pump -- ON
6. Emergency fuel valve -- check NORMAL
7. ECU SWAP -- ECU B (if no fix, return to AUTOMATIC)
8. If no improvement -- prepare for emergency landing per 3.5.1

### Restarting Engine -- Windmilling Propeller

Possible at 73--120 KIAS, below 6500 ft pressure altitude.

1. Airspeed for best glide -- 73/68/60 KIAS
2. Power lever -- MAX
3. Emergency fuel valve -- check NORMAL
4. Alternate air -- OPEN
5. Fuel transfer pump -- ON
6. AVIONIC MASTER -- OFF
7. ELECTRIC MASTER -- ON
8. Airspeed -- 73 to 120 KIAS
9. If unable to start -- glide configuration, emergency landing

### Restarting Engine -- Stationary Propeller

Possible below 6500 ft pressure altitude.

1. Airspeed for best glide -- 73/68/60 KIAS
2. ENGINE MASTER -- OFF
3. Power lever -- MAX
4. Emergency fuel valve -- check NORMAL
5. Alternate air -- OPEN
6. Fuel transfer pump -- ON
7. AVIONIC MASTER -- OFF
8. ELECTRIC MASTER -- ON
9. ENGINE MASTER -- ON (initiates glow cycle)
10. ELECTRIC MASTER -- START (release when engine running)

### Emergency Landing -- Engine Off

1. Select suitable landing area (upward slope if no level area)
2. Consider wind
3. Approach -- short-cut rectangular circuit
4. Airspeed -- 73 KIAS (1150 kg) / 68 KIAS (1000 kg) / 60 KIAS (850 kg)
5. Radio -- advise ATC
6. Emergency fuel valve -- OFF
7. ENGINE MASTER -- check OFF
8. When field assured: Flaps -- LDG
9. Safety harnesses -- tighten
10. ELECTRIC MASTER -- OFF (if time permits)
11. Touchdown -- lowest possible airspeed

### Smoke and Fire on Ground

1. Emergency fuel valve -- OFF
2. Fuel transfer pump -- OFF
3. ENGINE MASTER -- OFF
4. ELECTRIC MASTER -- OFF
5. After standstill: Canopy -- open
6. Evacuate immediately

### Smoke and Fire in Flight -- Engine Fire

1. Cabin heat -- OFF
2. Select emergency landing field
3. Emergency fuel valve -- OFF
4. Fuel transfer pump -- OFF
5. Cabin heat -- OFF
6. ENGINE MASTER -- OFF
7. ELECTRIC MASTER -- OFF
8. Emergency windows -- open if necessary
9. Carry out emergency landing with engine off

### Electrical Fire in Flight

1. EMERGENCY switch -- ON (if installed)
2. AVIONIC MASTER -- OFF
3. ELECTRIC MASTER -- OFF
4. Cabin heat -- OFF
5. Emergency windows -- open if required
6. Land at nearest appropriate airfield

### Spin Recovery

Steps 1-4 simultaneously and immediately:
1. Power lever -- IDLE
2. Rudder -- full deflection AGAINST direction of spin
3. Elevator (control stick) -- fully FORWARD
4. Ailerons -- NEUTRAL
5. Flaps -- UP
6. When rotation stops: Rudder -- neutral
7. Elevator -- pull carefully
8. Resume normal flight (do not exceed Vne 178 KIAS)

### Icing

1. Leave icing area (change altitude, turn back)
2. Pitot heating -- ON
3. Cabin heat -- ON
4. Air distributor lever -- DEFROST
5. Power lever -- increase power (prevent propeller ice)
6. Alternate air -- OPEN
7. Emergency windows -- open if required
8. ATC -- advise if emergency expected

### Gliding Configuration

1. Flaps -- UP
2. Airspeed -- 73 KIAS (1150 kg) / 68 KIAS (1000 kg) / 60 KIAS (850 kg)
3. Glide ratio 8.8:1 (windmilling) = 1.45 NM per 1000 ft lost
4. Glide ratio 10.3:1 (stationary prop) = 1.70 NM per 1000 ft lost

## Systems Summary

### Engine (TAE 125 Diesel)
The Thielert TAE 125 is a turbocharged, liquid-cooled, 4-cylinder diesel engine based on a Mercedes-Benz automotive diesel core. It burns JET A1 fuel and is controlled by a dual-channel FADEC (ECU A and ECU B). The single power lever controls both power and propeller RPM. The engine drives the propeller through a reduction gearbox.

### Fuel System
Two wing tanks (left and right) feed a central MAIN tank via gravity. Fuel flows from the MAIN tank to the engine. With Long Range Tanks installed, auxiliary (AUX) tank sections above the MAIN tanks hold additional fuel that must be transferred to the MAIN tanks via the electric fuel transfer pump. An emergency fuel valve allows mechanical fuel transfer from AUX to MAIN if the electric pump fails.

### Electrical System
14V DC system with engine-driven generator and main battery. Two buses: Essential Bus and Main Bus. Avionics on separate bus. ECU Backup Battery provides independent power to the FADEC in case of total electrical failure. Emergency switch (if installed) powers attitude gyro and flood light from a separate battery.

### Flight Controls
Conventional stick and rudder pedals. Electric trim (pitch). Flaps are electrically operated with three positions: UP, T/O, LDG. The T-tail configuration provides excellent pitch stability and spin resistance.

### Landing Gear
Fixed tricycle, nose-wheel steerable via rudder pedals. Hydraulic disc brakes on main wheels. No retraction mechanism. Track: 9 ft 9 in (2.97 m). Wheelbase: 5 ft 6 in (1.68 m).
