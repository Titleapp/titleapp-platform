## Normal Checklist (Abbreviated)

### Preflight Inspection

1. Documents -- ARROW check (Airworthiness, Registration, Radio license, Operating handbook, Weight & Balance)
2. Fuel quantity -- check visually, caps secure
3. Oil -- check (8--12 qt minimum for flight)
4. Walkaround -- complete (control surfaces, tires, pitot tube, antennas, cowl, exhaust, prop, gear)
5. Landing gear -- inspect struts, tires, brakes, door seals
6. Fuel selector -- LEFT or RIGHT
7. Master switch -- OFF
8. Mixture -- idle cutoff
9. Throttle -- closed
10. Propeller -- full INCREASE (high RPM)
11. Trim -- takeoff position

### Engine Start

1. Brakes -- set (parking brake)
2. Fuel selector -- LEFT (or fullest tank)
3. Electric fuel pump -- ON (low boost)
4. Mixture -- full RICH, then LEAN to just past peak
5. Mixture -- idle cutoff
6. Fuel pump -- OFF
7. Propeller -- full INCREASE
8. Throttle -- 1/4 in open
9. Master switch -- ON (BOTH)
10. Beacon -- ON
11. Ignition -- START (release when engine catches)
12. Mixture -- advance smoothly to RICH
13. Oil pressure -- green within 30 seconds
14. Throttle -- 1000 RPM (warm-up)
15. Fuel pump -- OFF (confirm steady fuel pressure)
16. Avionics master -- ON

### Before Takeoff (Run-Up)

1. Brakes -- hold
2. Throttle -- 1800 RPM
3. Magnetos -- check L, R, BOTH (max drop 150 RPM, max diff 50 RPM)
4. Propeller -- cycle (full decrease, then full increase), 2--3 times
5. Engine instruments -- all green
6. Throttle -- idle, check idle speed (575--650 RPM)
7. Throttle -- 1000 RPM
8. Flight instruments -- set (altimeter, DG, attitude)
9. Trim -- takeoff position
10. Fuel selector -- confirm (fullest tank)
11. Mixture -- RICH (or leaned for field elevation above 3000 ft)
12. Flaps -- set as desired
13. Doors -- closed and locked
14. Seatbelts -- secure
15. Controls -- free and correct

### Normal Takeoff

1. Flaps -- UP
2. Fuel pump -- LOW BOOST
3. Throttle -- full open smoothly
4. Prop -- full INCREASE (already set)
5. Mixture -- RICH
6. Engine instruments -- check (MP, RPM, fuel flow)
7. At 71 KIAS -- rotate
8. Positive rate -- gear UP
9. Climb at Vy (96 KIAS)
10. Above 500 ft AGL -- reduce to 25" MP / 2500 RPM (cruise climb)
11. Fuel pump -- OFF (above safe altitude)

### Short Field Takeoff

1. Flaps -- approach setting
2. Fuel pump -- LOW BOOST
3. Brakes -- hold, full power, check instruments
4. Brakes -- release
5. At 71 KIAS -- rotate
6. Climb at Vx (82 KIAS) until obstacle cleared
7. Gear -- UP after positive climb
8. Flaps -- retract above 100 KIAS
9. Accelerate to Vy (96 KIAS)

### Cruise

1. Altitude -- stabilize
2. Power -- set (typically 23" MP / 2300--2500 RPM for 65% power)
3. Mixture -- lean for best economy or best power (peak EGT or 50 deg F rich of peak)
4. Trim -- adjust
5. Fuel selector -- switch tanks every 30 minutes
6. Fuel pump -- OFF
7. Engine instruments -- monitor

### Approach and Landing

1. ATIS -- obtain
2. Fuel selector -- fullest tank
3. Mixture -- RICH (or set for field elevation)
4. Propeller -- full INCREASE (2700 RPM)
5. Fuel pump -- LOW BOOST
6. Below Vle (152 KIAS) -- gear DOWN, 3 green lights
7. Flaps -- approach setting
8. Airspeed -- 90 KIAS on downwind, 85 on base
9. Flaps -- full on final
10. Airspeed -- 80 KIAS on final (76 KIAS short field)
11. Trim -- adjust
12. Touchdown -- main gear first, hold nosewheel off

### Short Field Landing

1. Fuel pump -- LOW BOOST
2. Gear -- DOWN
3. Full flaps
4. Airspeed -- 76 KIAS over obstacle
5. Power -- as needed for glidepath
6. Touchdown -- minimum speed, main gear first
7. Brakes -- maximum
8. Flaps -- UP (dump lift)

### After Landing

1. Flaps -- UP
2. Trim -- takeoff position
3. Fuel pump -- OFF
4. Transponder -- STBY

### Engine Shutdown

1. Avionics master -- OFF
2. Throttle -- 1000 RPM (cool-down 2 min recommended)
3. Mixture -- idle cutoff (lean to stop)
4. Ignition -- OFF (after engine stops)
5. Master switch -- OFF
6. Fuel selector -- OFF
7. Control lock -- install

## Emergency Checklist

### Engine Failure on Takeoff (low altitude)

1. Airspeed -- 105 KIAS (best glide)
2. Mixture -- idle cutoff
3. Fuel selector -- OFF
4. Ignition -- OFF
5. Master switch -- OFF (unless needed for gear/flaps)
6. Gear -- leave DOWN if already extended; if retracted, evaluate
7. Flaps -- as required for field
8. Land straight ahead (do NOT attempt return to runway below 1000 ft AGL)

### Engine Failure in Flight

1. Airspeed -- 105 KIAS (best glide)
2. Fuel selector -- switch tanks
3. Fuel pump -- ON (high boost)
4. Mixture -- RICH
5. Ignition -- check BOTH, then try L, then R
6. Throttle -- advance slowly
7. If no restart, execute forced landing
8. Mayday -- 121.5 MHz, transponder 7700

### Engine Partial Power Loss

1. Mixture -- RICH
2. Fuel pump -- ON (high boost)
3. Fuel selector -- switch tanks
4. Throttle -- check for smooth operation
5. Magnetos -- check L and R separately
6. If partial power maintained -- divert to nearest airfield
7. If power continues to deteriorate -- prepare for forced landing

### Engine Fire on Ground

1. Mixture -- idle cutoff
2. Throttle -- full open (draw fire into engine)
3. Fuel selector -- OFF
4. Fuel pump -- OFF
5. Master switch -- OFF
6. Evacuate
7. Extinguisher -- use if available

### Engine Fire in Flight

1. Mixture -- idle cutoff
2. Fuel selector -- OFF
3. Fuel pump -- OFF
4. Master switch -- OFF
5. Cabin heat/vents -- close firewall shutoff
6. Airspeed -- increase to 120 KIAS (sideslip to direct flames away)
7. Execute emergency landing immediately

### Electrical Fire in Flight

1. Master switch -- OFF
2. Avionics master -- OFF
3. All electrical switches -- OFF
4. Vents/cabin air -- open (ventilate smoke)
5. Extinguisher -- use on visible fire
6. If fire out -- master ON, check circuits one at a time
7. Land as soon as practical

### Landing Gear Failure to Extend

1. Airspeed -- reduce below Vle (152 KIAS)
2. Gear selector -- cycle DOWN
3. Check gear lights and mirror (visual confirm)
4. If gear still not down -- emergency extension:
   a. Gear selector -- DOWN
   b. Emergency gear release handle -- PULL
   c. Gear extends by gravity
5. Confirm 3 green lights
6. If partial gear -- plan belly landing on foam-equipped runway if available
7. Notify ATC, request emergency equipment

### Loss of Oil Pressure

1. Land as soon as possible
2. Select nearest suitable field
3. Power -- reduce to minimum needed
4. Monitor oil temperature -- rising temp confirms oil loss
5. Prepare for engine seizure / forced landing

### Spin Recovery (PARE)

1. Power -- idle
2. Ailerons -- neutral
3. Rudder -- full opposite to spin direction
4. Elevator -- briskly forward through neutral
5. Rudder -- neutral when rotation stops
6. Wings -- level, recover from dive (do not exceed Vne)

### Vacuum System Failure

1. Confirm failure (suction gauge zero or flag)
2. Attitude indicator and directional gyro -- UNRELIABLE
3. Use turn coordinator, magnetic compass, GPS for navigation
4. Advise ATC, request vectors if IMC
5. Engage autopilot electric backup (if equipped)

## Systems Summary

### Engine (IO-550-B)
6-cylinder, fuel-injected, horizontally opposed, air-cooled. 300 hp at 2700 RPM. Controlled by throttle (manifold pressure), propeller (RPM), and mixture. No carburetor (fuel-injected). Dual magneto ignition with impulse coupling for starting. Oil cooler with vernacular-controlled flap.

### Fuel System
Two wing tanks (37 gal each, all usable). Fuel selector: LEFT, RIGHT, OFF. No BOTH position -- must manually switch tanks approximately every 30 minutes. Engine-driven fuel pump with electric boost pump (low and high modes). Fuel injection system. Fuel totalizer on G1000.

### Electrical System
28V DC, engine-driven 60-amp alternator + 24V battery. Master switch has two halves (ALT and BAT). Avionics on dedicated avionics master bus. Over-voltage relay protects avionics. Standby battery available on G1000-equipped models.

### Propeller
Hartzell 3-blade constant speed. Prop lever controls governor: full forward = high RPM (fine pitch), full aft = low RPM (coarse pitch). Oil pressure drives to low pitch; counterweights and spring drive to high pitch (feather direction). Not a feathering prop on standard Bonanza.

### Flight Controls
Conventional yoke and rudder pedals. Electrically actuated flaps (approach and full down positions). Electric pitch trim with manual backup. Rudder trim (manual). Aileron trim not installed. Yoke-mounted electric trim switches.

### Landing Gear
Retractable tricycle gear, electrically actuated. Emergency extension via manual release handle (gravity drop). Gear position indicated by 3 green lights (down and locked). Gear warning horn sounds if gear not down with throttle below approximately 12" MP. Squat switch prevents retraction on ground.
