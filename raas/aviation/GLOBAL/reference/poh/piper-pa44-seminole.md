## 8. Systems Summary

### Electrical
- 2x engine-driven alternators (28V)
- 24V battery (emergency battery provides ~30 min for essential instruments)
- Alternator output: max 60A ground, 75A flight
- IFR not recommended on single alternator

### Landing Gear
- Hydraulically actuated retractable tricycle gear
- No mechanical uplocks (gear free-falls to down position if hydraulic failure)
- Gear down airspeed approximately 75% of gear-retracted airspeed at same power setting
- 3 green lights confirm gear down and locked (also displayed on G1000 MFD)
- Nacelle mirror on left engine for visual nose gear verification
- CHECK GEAR CAS alert: mutable above ~400 ft AGL, warning below ~400 ft AGL

### Avionics
- Garmin G1000 Integrated Flight Deck (PFD + MFD)
- GFC 700 autopilot (autopilot coupled go-around NOT authorized during single-engine operations)
- Aspen EFD-1000 standby instrument (internal battery backup)
- Dual AHRS, dual ADC, GPS WAAS
- GNSS approved: GPS approaches (LNAV, LNAV/VNAV, LPV, LP)

### Propellers
- Counter-rotating constant speed (no critical engine)
- Zero-thrust RPM: 1850 at 82 KIAS (Vsse), 2180 at 88 KIAS (Vyse)
- Feathering: Full aft propeller control position
- Governor check: retard propeller until 100-200 RPM drop, then advance throttle

### Fuel System
- 2 independent nacelle fuel tanks (55 gal each, 54 usable)
- Crossfeed available for fuel balancing
- Fuel pumps: engine-driven + electric boost (ON for takeoff, landing, and altitude operations)

## 10. Operating Limitations

- Flight in known icing conditions is prohibited.
- Aerobatic maneuvers (including spins) are prohibited.
- Maximum operating altitude: limited by single-engine service ceiling considerations.
- Autopilot coupled go-around is not authorized during single-engine operations.
- One-engine-inoperative stalls are not recommended.
- Do not fly below Vmca (56 KIAS) with one engine inoperative.
- Power-off stall with gear and flaps retracted may result in altitude loss of up to 300 feet.
- Noise level: 74.7 dB(A) with two-blade propeller (FAR 36 compliant).

## 11. Single-Engine Procedures

### Critical Speeds
- Vmca: 56 KIAS (red radial line -- minimum controllable airspeed, one engine inoperative)
- Vsse: 82 KIAS (intentional one-engine inoperative speed -- recommended minimum)
- Vyse: 88 KIAS (best rate of climb, single engine -- blue line)
- Vxse: 82 KIAS (best angle of climb, single engine)

### Engine Failure After Takeoff
1. Mixtures: FULL RICH
2. Propellers: FULL INCREASE
3. Throttles: FULL FORWARD
4. Flaps: UP
5. Landing Gear: UP
6. Identify: dead foot = dead engine
7. Verify: retard throttle on suspected engine
8. Confirm: feather inoperative propeller
9. Bank: 2-3 degrees toward operating engine
10. Airspeed: maintain Vyse (88 KIAS) or above
11. Cowl flap (inop engine): CLOSED
12. Fuel pump (inop engine): OFF
13. Fuel selector (inop engine): OFF

### Vmca Demonstration (FAA Flight Test)
- Gear up, flaps up, altitude at or above 4000 ft AGL
- Airspeed at or above 82 KIAS (Vsse)
- Simulated inop engine at idle, other at full power
- Reduce airspeed ~1 kt/sec until Vmca indicators or stall warning
- Immediately recover: reduce power to idle, lower nose to regain Vmca, accelerate to Vsse

### Single-Engine Climb Performance (Sea Level, ISA, 3800 lb)
- Rate of climb: approximately 205 FPM (gear up, prop feathered, Vyse 88 KIAS)
- Performance degrades significantly with altitude and above-standard temperature.
- At density altitudes above approximately 3800 ft, single-engine climb capability may be near zero.
