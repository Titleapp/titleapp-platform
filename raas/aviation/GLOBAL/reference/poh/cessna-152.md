## Emergency Procedures Summary

### Engine Failure in Flight
1. Airspeed: 60 KIAS (best glide)
2. Mixture: RICH
3. Carburetor Heat: ON
4. Primer: IN and LOCKED
5. Fuel Shutoff Valve: ON
6. Ignition Switch: BOTH (or START if propeller stopped)
7. Master Switch: ON

### Spin Recovery
1. Verify ailerons are neutral and throttle is in idle position
2. Apply and HOLD full rudder opposite to the direction of rotation
3. Just AFTER the rudder reaches the stop, move the control wheel BRISKLY forward far enough to break the stall
4. HOLD these control inputs until rotation stops
5. As rotation stops, neutralize rudder and make a smooth recovery from the resulting dive

Notes: For training, 1 to 2 turn spins are adequate. At least 1,000 ft altitude loss per turn. Minimum entry altitude for 1-turn spin: 4,000 ft AGL. Spins with flaps extended are prohibited. Spins with baggage or occupied child's seat are not approved. Must be within utility category W&B limits.

### Forced Landing (Engine Failure After Takeoff)
1. Airspeed: 55 KIAS (flaps up), 50 KIAS (flaps down)
2. Mixture: IDLE CUT OFF
3. Fuel Shutoff Valve: OFF
4. Ignition Switch: OFF
5. Wing Flaps: As required
6. Master Switch: OFF

### Engine Fire During Start
1. Cranking: Continue to get fire into engine
2. If engine starts: Run at 1700 RPM for a few minutes
3. If engine does not start: Throttle FULL, Mixture IDLE CUT OFF, Crank engine, Fire extinguisher

## Operating Limitations

### Approved Maneuvers (Utility Category)
All maneuvers approved for utility category single-engine aircraft, including spins (within W&B limits). Spins are approved only with dual instruction from a qualified instructor familiar with the Cessna 152 spin characteristics.

### General Limitations
- Flight into known icing conditions: Prohibited
- Aerobatic flight beyond utility category approved maneuvers: Prohibited
- Maximum operating altitude: ~14,700 ft (service ceiling)
- Maximum demonstrated crosswind component: 12 knots
- Carburetor heat preheat required below -18 deg C (0 deg F), recommended below -7 deg C (20 deg F)
- Cruising at 65--75% power for first 50 hours or after cylinder work (engine break-in)

## Systems Summary

### Electrical
- 14-volt DC system
- 60-amp belt-driven alternator
- 12-volt battery
- Master switch controls both battery and alternator

### Fuel System
- Gravity feed from two wing tanks
- Left/Right/Both selector valve
- Carburetor with carburetor heat
- Fuel strainer with drain

### Flight Controls
- Conventional cable-actuated ailerons, elevator, rudder
- Manual flap control (0, 10, 20, 30 degrees)
- Manual elevator trim wheel

### Ignition
- Dual magneto system (Slick or Bendix)
- Magneto check at 1700 RPM: max drop 125 RPM, max differential 50 RPM
