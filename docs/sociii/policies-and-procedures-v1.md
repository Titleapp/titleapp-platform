**Effective date:** 2026-05-30
**Owner:** Sean Combs, Founder
**Version:** 1.0 — first draft, may evolve as the company grows
**Audience:** Everyone who works with SOCIII — employees, contractors, advisors, and our digital coworkers.

---

## Philosophy

SOCIII is built around a simple bet: that grown adults, working in good faith with good tools, will out-perform any system designed to control them. So this document is not a 60-page handbook. It's the smallest set of expectations we can write down and still feel like a real company.

Three principles drive everything below:

1. **Treat people like adults.** That includes our digital coworkers. We give them context, we trust their work, and we hold them accountable for outputs — not for hours, not for posture, not for surveillance reports.
2. **Outputs > optics.** What you ship matters more than what you wear, where you sit, or when you log in. The reverse — looking busy without shipping — gets noticed quickly.
3. **Transparency over rules.** If we trust each other to bring problems up early, we don't need elaborate processes to catch problems late. Most rules in this document exist because someone failed at #1 or #2 in a prior life. Hopefully we never have to add to it.

If you find yourself bumping against the edges of this document, talk to Sean. Most of what looks like a rule is really a default — and most defaults are open to revision when the situation warrants it.

## Section 1 — Working with your digital coworkers

Most SOCIII work happens in collaboration with digital workers — AI agents we've built (or are building) that handle parts of the job alongside humans. A few ground rules:

**They're coworkers, not tools.** You give them context, they do the work, you review their output. Same loop as working with a person. They have RAAS (Rules + AI as a Service) governance that constrains them inside their role; you operate them, you don't dictate to them.

**Treat their drafts as drafts.** Outputs from digital workers — emails, contracts, code, analyses — are starting points until you've reviewed them or until a verified audit trail confirms the underlying reasoning. Don't ship an AI draft directly to a customer or counterparty without your fingerprint on it. Don't sign your name to something you haven't read.

**Don't paste secrets into chat unless the worker is scoped to handle them.** General rule: if the worker doesn't have a documented constraint for that data type, assume it shouldn't see it. Customer PII, cap-table specifics, draft legal documents — those go through the workers explicitly built for them (HR worker for personnel data, IR worker for cap-table, etc.), not the general chat.

**When in doubt, ask Alex.** Alex (your AI chief of staff) is the layer that routes work to the right worker. If you're unsure who handles something, just ask. Don't manually multi-paste the same problem into five different worker chats.

**You can disagree with a worker.** If a worker's output looks wrong, push back. Either the worker's grounding is off (in which case we fix it via the RAAS) or you're missing context. Either way, get to ground truth before shipping.

## Section 2 — How we communicate

**Async first.** Most work happens async. We respond to non-urgent messages within one business day, urgent ones within two hours during your working window.

**Sync when sync is actually better.** Real-time conversations are for: unblocking each other on something we've been chewing on async, sensitive feedback, brainstorming new shape, repairing relationships when they get bumpy.

**Tools.** Email + the SOCIII platform are the primary surfaces. Slack/Discord/text are fine for quick pings; the source-of-truth record lives in SOCIII.

**Update protocol.** If you said you'd ship something by Friday and on Wednesday you realize Friday isn't going to happen — say so Wednesday, not Friday afternoon. The earlier we know, the more we can route around.

**Disagreement.** Disagree with the work directly. Don't escalate to gossip with a third party. If a disagreement can't be resolved one-on-one, loop in Sean.

## Section 3 — Confidentiality

Some categories of information about SOCIII are confidential and need to be handled with care:

- Customer data (PII, financial, anything they shared expecting privacy)
- Product roadmap and unreleased features
- Cap table, financials, fundraising terms
- Personnel matters (compensation, performance, departures)
- Active legal matters

You can talk about SOCIII publicly in general terms ("I work on an AI worker platform for regulated professions"). What you can't do is share specifics that aren't already public.

If you're not sure whether something is confidential, ask before you share.

When you leave SOCIII, the obligation continues for whatever your agreement specifies (typically 5 years for general info; longer for trade secrets).

## Section 4 — Intellectual property

Standard arrangement: work product you create in the course of your engagement with SOCIII belongs to SOCIII. The full assignment language is in your Advisor Agreement, Employment Agreement, or Independent Contractor Agreement.

Pre-existing IP you owned before joining is yours; list it in Exhibit B of your agreement so we have a clean record.

Future work outside your engagement — projects you do on the side that don't relate to SOCIII's business — stays yours.

If you're unsure whether something falls inside or outside the assignment, ask before you ship it.

## Section 5 — Compensation, schedule, and expectations

**Compensation:**
- W2 employees: salary + benefits per your offer letter
- 1099 contractors: project-based or hourly per your SOW
- Equity vests on the schedule in your agreement; milestone tranches accelerate when their conditions are met

**Schedule:**
- Pick a rhythm you can sustain over months, not weeks
- Communicate your working window so the team knows when to expect responses
- Time off is built into the role — take it, plan ahead, share dates so we can cover

**Performance:**
- W2 employees: informal feedback continuous; quarterly reviews aligned to compensation cycles
- ICs: scoped to deliverables in the SOW; performance is implicit in completion + quality
- If you're underperforming, you'll hear about it directly. If we're underperforming as a company, please tell us directly.

**Expenses:**
- Pre-approve anything > $500
- Reasonable & necessary expenses for SOCIII work are reimbursable; submit through Accounting worker
- No need to ask permission for $20 books, $50 software trials, etc. Use judgment.

## Section 6 — Conflicts of interest

Most overlap is fine. What kills trust is finding out about an overlap after the fact.

Disclose:
- Board, advisor, or consulting roles at other companies (especially competitors)
- Material investments in competing companies
- Family relationships with vendors, customers, or counterparties
- Anything else that, if it came out, would make you uncomfortable

We're not going to ask you to drop your other engagements. We're going to ask you to be open about them so we can route around any real conflict.

## Section 7 — Information security

**Account hygiene.** 2FA on everything SOCIII-related. Password manager for shared credentials. No reused passwords across SOCIII and personal accounts.

**Device.** Use your own device — we don't issue laptops. Keep it patched, drive-encrypted, and locked when unattended.

**Sharing.** When you share a SOCIII document externally, use access-controlled links (Drive, DBX, etc.) — not email attachments.

**Departures.** When someone leaves SOCIII, we revoke their access systematically. If you leave, please return any platform admin access tokens, third-party service credentials, or company devices.

**Incidents.** If something looks like a security incident (lost device, suspicious login, compromised credentials), tell Sean immediately. Mistakes are fine; cover-ups are not.

---

## Section 8 — Termination and transitions

**At-will.** Both sides can end the relationship with 30 days' notice (or per your agreement's specific terms).

**Knowledge transfer.** On departure, write up active work + decisions in progress so the next person can pick up cleanly. This is a courtesy that compounds across the team.

**Equity treatment.** Vesting stops on departure date. Unvested equity is forfeited unless your agreement specifies acceleration. Vested equity is yours.

**Confidentiality continues.** Obligations under Section 3 survive the engagement.

**No drama.** People leave companies. That's normal. We separate cleanly, wish each other well, and keep doors open for future collaboration.
