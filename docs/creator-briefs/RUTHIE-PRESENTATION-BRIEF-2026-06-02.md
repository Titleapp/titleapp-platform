## The economics (have these numbers ready)

- **For students:** $29/month → access to expert nursing knowledge that would cost $200/hr to consult clinically
- **For creators (Ruthie):** 75% of every subscription. If 200 nursing students subscribe, that's ~$5,000/month in passive income to the creator
- **For the school:** Optional revenue share + a recruiting story ("our program produces nurses who can also build digital workers")
- **Platform take rate:** 25%
- **Worker production cost:** ~$1-5 per explainer video (Kling AI gen), audited workflow data
- **No card required to try:** 7-day free trial, cancel from chat

## The three asks (in priority order)

1. **Approval to develop the worker as part of your curriculum / clinical-judgment work.** This is the low-bar ask — you're already doing the domain work; SOCIII just packages it.

2. **Permission to invite 10-20 students to beta-test the worker** as it develops. Free for them; their feedback shapes v1. No clinical liability — the worker is a study/decision-support tool, attestation framing.

3. **(Stretch)** Explore the worker as a school-affiliated product where the school takes a small revenue share. Could be a recurring revenue stream the program controls — unusual for a nursing school.

## Why now, why SOCIII

- **The platform exists.** Most education innovation pitches are "imagine if we had…" — this is "log in and try it right now."
- **First-mover advantage in nursing.** No other nursing program has a faculty-owned digital worker. Whoever does this first owns the category.
- **No tech overhead.** Ruthie writes the clinical content; the platform handles backend, billing, distribution, video production, marketing. The school doesn't take on technical risk.
- **Sean (the platform founder) is a medevac pilot** who built the platform between shifts. The whole story is "domain experts shipping software" — Ruthie's project IS the company's thesis.

## If they ask the hard questions

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| *What about clinical liability?* | The worker is positioned as study + decision-support. Every output is attestation-flagged: "verify with your clinical judgment / preceptor." This is consistent with how nursing education already uses simulation + decision-support tools. |
| *How is this different from ChatGPT?* | ChatGPT is generic and has no domain accountability. The Ruthie worker is built on a specific knowledge base, rule-set, and identity. Subscribers know they're getting *your* expertise — not a probability cloud. |
| *What's the technical risk to me?* | None. Ruthie does no coding. The platform absorbs infrastructure, security, billing, support. The school's risk surface is editorial review — same as approving any curriculum content. |
| *How does the school benefit?* | (1) Innovation story to share with prospective students. (2) Optional revenue share. (3) Faculty-built tools that students can actually use. (4) An on-ramp for students who want to build their own workers — a new kind of nursing-meets-tech career path. |
| *Could the worker replace nursing instruction?* | No — and that's not the goal. The worker is 24/7 supplemental access to the kind of senior-nurse mentorship that's bottlenecked at every clinical site. It augments the instructor, doesn't replace them. |
| *What about HIPAA / patient privacy?* | The worker doesn't handle patient data. It's an educational + clinical-reasoning tool. We have a Madison/HIPAA Compliance worker for the institutional side if needed. |
