# INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT

This Independent Contractor Agreement (this "**Agreement**") is entered into as of {{Effective Date}} (the "**Effective Date**") by and between **SOCIII, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 1810 E Sahara Ave STE 75942, Las Vegas, NV 89104 (the "**Company**"), and **{{Contractor Name}}**, an individual (or entity) residing (or organized) at {{Contractor Address}} (the "**Contractor**"). The Company and the Contractor are each referred to herein as a "**Party**" and collectively as the "**Parties**."

## 1. Services

1.1 **Scope.** The Contractor shall provide to the Company the services described in **Exhibit A: Scope of Services** (the "**Services**"), as may be amended or supplemented from time to time by the mutual written agreement of the Parties.

1.2 **Standard of Performance.** The Contractor shall perform the Services in a professional, workmanlike, and timely manner consistent with industry standards applicable to the type of services rendered.

1.3 **Cooperation.** The Contractor shall reasonably cooperate with the Company's personnel and shall comply with the Company's reasonable policies and procedures made known to the Contractor in writing, provided that such policies shall not alter the Contractor's status as an independent contractor.

## 2. Compensation

2.1 **Compensation Structure.** The Company shall compensate the Contractor as set forth in **Exhibit B: Compensation**. Exhibit B may include any combination of the following components, as specified therein:

(a) a fixed cash retainer in the amount of {{retainer_amount}} payable on a {{retainer_frequency}} basis;

(b) hourly fees billed at {{hourly_rate}} per hour, with invoices submitted no more frequently than monthly and supported by reasonable time records;

(c) success fees equal to {{success_fee_pct}}% of {{success_fee_basis}}, payable upon the occurrence and only upon the occurrence of the events described in Exhibit B;

(d) milestone-vested equity in the form of {{equity_instrument}} representing {{equity_pct}}% of the Company's fully-diluted common stock as of the Effective Date, subject to the vesting schedule, performance milestones, and forfeiture conditions set forth in Exhibit B and to the terms of the Company's equity incentive plan and the applicable award agreement; and

(e) reimbursement of reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, supported by customary documentation and submitted within thirty (30) days of being incurred.

2.2 **Invoicing and Payment.** Unless otherwise specified in Exhibit B, the Contractor shall submit invoices to the Company on a monthly basis, and the Company shall pay undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days of receipt.

2.3 **Taxes.** The Contractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes, including self-employment taxes, arising from the Compensation. The Company shall issue an IRS Form 1099 (or successor form) to the Contractor as required by law.

2.4 **No Other Compensation.** Except as expressly set forth in Exhibit B, the Contractor shall not be entitled to any other compensation, commission, bonus, equity, or benefits from the Company.

## 3. Term and Termination

3.1 **Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of {{initial_term_months}} months (the "**Initial Term**"), and shall automatically renew for successive {{renewal_months}}-month renewal terms (each, a "**Renewal Term**" and, together with the Initial Term, the "**Term**") unless either Party gives the other Party at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice of non-renewal.

3.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

3.3 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

3.4 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement: (a) the Contractor shall promptly deliver to the Company all work product, Company materials, and Confidential Information then in the Contractor's possession or control; (b) the Company shall pay the Contractor for Services properly performed and undisputed expenses properly incurred through the effective date of termination; and (c) the rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections 4 through 12 shall survive termination of this Agreement in accordance with their terms.

3.5 **Survival of Equity and Success Fees.** Treatment of unvested equity and any earned but unpaid success fees upon termination shall be governed exclusively by Exhibit B and the applicable award documents.

## 4. Independent Contractor Status

4.1 **No Employment Relationship.** The Contractor is an independent contractor and is not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee relationship.

4.2 **No Authority to Bind.** The Contractor has no authority to bind, contract on behalf of, or make representations or commitments on behalf of the Company, except as the Company may expressly authorize in writing in advance.

4.3 **Means and Methods.** The Contractor shall control the means, manner, and methods by which the Services are performed, subject to the requirement that the Services satisfy the deliverables, milestones, and standards set forth in this Agreement and in Exhibit A.

4.4 **No Benefits.** The Contractor is not entitled to and waives any claim to participate in any Company employee benefit plan, including health insurance, paid time off, retirement, disability, workers' compensation, or unemployment insurance.

4.5 **Insurance.** The Contractor shall maintain, at the Contractor's own expense, such liability, errors and omissions, and other insurance as is commercially reasonable for the nature, scope, and risk profile of the Services rendered, and shall provide certificates of insurance upon the Company's reasonable request.

## 5. Intellectual Property Assignment

5.1 **Work Product.** All deliverables, work product, materials, documents, designs, code, data, inventions, discoveries, improvements, processes, methodologies, and other results created, conceived, developed, or first reduced to practice by the Contractor (alone or with others) in the course of performing the Services (collectively, "**Work Product**") shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

5.2 **Work Made for Hire.** To the maximum extent permitted by law, the Work Product shall be deemed a "work made for hire" within the meaning of the U.S. Copyright Act and other applicable law.

5.3 **Assignment.** To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as a work made for hire, or to the extent the Contractor retains any right, title, or interest in any Work Product, the Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company, without further consideration, all of the Contractor's right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including all patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, and other intellectual property rights worldwide.

5.4 **Pre-Existing IP.** "**Pre-Existing IP**" means intellectual property owned by the Contractor prior to the Effective Date, or developed by the Contractor wholly outside the scope of the Services and without use of any Company resources, and listed in **Exhibit C: Pre-Existing IP** (default: none). The Contractor retains ownership of Pre-Existing IP but hereby grants the Company a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free, fully paid-up, sublicensable license to use Pre-Existing IP solely to the extent incorporated into any Work Product or necessary for the Company to exploit the Work Product.

5.5 **Further Assurances.** The Contractor shall execute such further documents and take such further actions as the Company may reasonably request to perfect, record, or enforce the Company's rights in the Work Product, at the Company's expense.

5.6 **Moral Rights.** To the extent the Contractor retains any "moral rights" or similar rights in the Work Product, the Contractor hereby irrevocably waives such rights in favor of the Company.

## 6. Confidentiality

6.1 **Confidential Information.** "**Confidential Information**" means all non-public information of the Company disclosed to or learned by the Contractor in connection with this Agreement, including without limitation technical information, source code, algorithms, product roadmaps, business plans, financial information, investor information, customer and prospect information, supplier information, pricing, personnel information, strategic plans, and any other information that is marked as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential under the circumstances.

6.2 **Obligations.** The Contractor shall: (a) hold the Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the Services; (c) not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party without the Company's prior written consent; and (d) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care the Contractor uses to protect the Contractor's own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

6.3 **Exceptions.** The obligations in Section 6.2 do not apply to information that: (a) was lawfully in the Contractor's possession without restriction before disclosure by the Company; (b) becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement; (c) is independently developed by the Contractor without use of or reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or governmental authority, provided that the Contractor gives the Company prompt written notice (where legally permitted) and reasonable cooperation to seek a protective order.

6.4 **Survival.** The Contractor's obligations under this Section 6 shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years, except that obligations with respect to information that constitutes a trade secret shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

## 7. Non-Solicitation

7.1 **Personnel.** During the Term and for twelve (12) months thereafter, the Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit for employment or engagement, or otherwise encourage to terminate their relationship with the Company, any employee, independent contractor, or service provider of the Company with whom the Contractor had material contact during the Term. General solicitations not specifically directed at Company personnel (such as job postings on public job boards) shall not violate this Section 7.

7.2 **No Non-Compete.** The Parties expressly acknowledge that this Agreement does not contain a non-competition covenant. The Contractor remains free to engage in other business activities, including activities that may be competitive with the Company, subject to the Contractor's obligations under Section 6 (Confidentiality), Section 5 (Intellectual Property Assignment), and Section 8 (Representations and Warranties).

## 8. Representations and Warranties

8.1 **Contractor Representations.** The Contractor represents and warrants that: (a) the Contractor has full power and authority to enter into and perform this Agreement; (b) the Contractor's performance of the Services does not and will not conflict with or violate any other agreement, obligation, or duty owed by the Contractor to any third party; (c) the Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards; (d) the Work Product will be original to the Contractor (except for Pre-Existing IP and properly licensed third-party materials disclosed to the Company in writing) and will not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party; (e) the Contractor will comply with all laws, rules, and regulations applicable to the performance of the Services, including without limitation, where applicable, federal and state securities laws, anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws, and data protection laws; and (f) the Contractor has not been debarred, suspended, or otherwise determined ineligible to participate in federal or state programs.

## 9. Securities Law Compliance

9.1 **Applicability.** This Section 9 applies whenever the Services include, in whole or in part, fundraising assistance, investor introductions, capital markets services, or other activities relating to the offer or sale of securities of the Company or its affiliates (collectively, "**Capital Raise Services**").

9.2 **Not a Broker-Dealer or Finder.** The Contractor represents and warrants that the Contractor is not, and will not be required to be, registered as a broker-dealer, finder, investment adviser, or municipal advisor under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, the Investment Advisers Act of 1940, or any analogous state law in connection with the Services, and that the Services as performed will not require any such registration.

9.3 **Scope of Permitted Activity.** In rendering Capital Raise Services, the Contractor shall: (a) not negotiate the terms of any securities transaction on behalf of the Company; (b) not handle, receive, or disburse investor funds or securities; (c) not provide investment advice or make recommendations regarding the purchase, sale, or valuation of securities; (d) not engage in general solicitation or general advertising in a manner that would jeopardize an exemption from registration relied upon by the Company; and (e) limit the Contractor's role to making introductions, providing administrative and logistical support, and facilitating communications between the Company and prospective investors at the Company's direction.

9.4 **Success Fees.** The Parties acknowledge that the payment of transaction-based compensation (including success fees calculated as a percentage of capital raised) to unregistered persons is subject to scrutiny under federal and state securities laws. Any success fees payable under Exhibit B are paid in consideration of the introduction, administrative, and facilitation services described in Section 9.3 and not in consideration of soliciting investors, effecting transactions in securities, or otherwise engaging in activity requiring broker-dealer registration. The Parties further acknowledge that the structuring of such fees has been reviewed by counsel to the Company, and the Contractor has been advised to seek independent counsel regarding the Contractor's own regulatory status.

9.5 **Cooperation; Disclosure.** The Contractor shall (a) provide the Company with such information as the Company reasonably requests in connection with the Company's compliance with securities laws, including questionnaires regarding the Contractor's status and prior regulatory history, and (b) promptly disclose to the Company any subpoena, inquiry, or investigation by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority, or any state securities regulator that relates to the Services or the Company.

9.6 **Company Acknowledgment.** The Company acknowledges that broker-dealer regulations apply to capital raising activities and that the Company bears ultimate responsibility for the lawful conduct of its securities offerings.

## 10. Indemnification

10.1 **By Contractor.** The Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company, its affiliates, and their respective officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all third-party losses, damages, liabilities, judgments, settlements, fines, penalties, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) the Contractor's material breach of this Agreement; (b) the Contractor's negligence, gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud; (c) any claim that the Work Product or any Pre-Existing IP licensed hereunder infringes or misappropriates the intellectual property rights of any third party; (d) any claim by a taxing authority that the Contractor was misclassified due to facts within the Contractor's control; or (e) the Contractor's violation of any applicable law in the performance of the Services.

10.2 **Procedure.** The Company shall promptly notify the Contractor of any claim subject to indemnification, tender control of the defense to the Contractor (subject to the Company's right to participate at its own expense), and provide reasonable cooperation. The Contractor shall not settle any claim that imposes any non-monetary obligation on the Company or admits Company liability without the Company's prior written consent.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

11.1 **Cap.** EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SECTION 11.2, EACH PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE BY THE COMPANY TO THE CONTRACTOR UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

11.2 **Exclusions.** The cap in Section 11.1 does not apply to: (a) the Contractor's indemnification obligations under Section 10.1(c) (IP infringement); (b) either Party's breach of Section 6 (Confidentiality); (c) the Contractor's gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud; (d) the Contractor's violation of Section 9 (Securities Law Compliance); or (e) amounts owed under Section 2 (Compensation).

11.3 **No Consequential Damages.** EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SECTION 11.2, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS OR REVENUES, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY THEREOF.

## 12. Miscellaneous

12.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

12.2 **Jurisdiction and Venue.** The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in the State of Delaware for any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, and waive any objection to such venue.

12.3 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with its Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous agreements, representations, and understandings, whether written or oral.

12.4 **Amendments.** No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

12.5 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect, and the invalid or unenforceable provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the Parties' intent.

12.6 **Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that or any other provision.

12.7 **Counterparts; Electronic Signature.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Signatures delivered electronically (including via DocuSign, Dropbox Sign, or PDF) shall be deemed originals.

12.8 **Assignment.** The Contractor may not assign this Agreement, in whole or in part, without the Company's prior written consent. The Company may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets, without the Contractor's consent. Any attempted assignment in violation of this Section is void.

12.9 **Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email (with confirmation of receipt) or by overnight courier to the addresses set forth above (or such other addresses as a Party may designate in writing). Notice is deemed given upon receipt.

12.10 **Headings.** Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

12.11 **Independent Counsel.** Each Party acknowledges that it has had the opportunity to consult with independent legal counsel of its choice in connection with this Agreement, or has knowingly waived such opportunity.
