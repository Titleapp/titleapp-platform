This Contributor Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into by and between **SOCIII, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 1810 E Sahara Ave STE 75942, Las Vegas, NV 89104 (the "Company"), and **{{contributor_name}}**, an individual residing at the address on file with the Company and reachable at {{contributor_email}} (the "Contributor"), effective as of {{agreement_date}} (the "Effective Date"). ## 1. Worker Authorship

1.1 **Scope of Engagement.** The Contributor will author, configure, and maintain one or more **Digital Workers** (each, a "Worker," and collectively, the "Workers") for publication on the SOCIII platform (the "Platform"). Each Worker consists of a defined Ruleset, domain knowledge, prompts, configuration metadata, and any supporting materials necessary for the Worker to operate within the Platform's runtime environment.

1.2 **Company Acceptance.** No Worker authored by the Contributor will be published to the SOCIII marketplace (the "Marketplace") without the Company's prior written acceptance, which acceptance may be granted, conditioned, or withheld in the Company's reasonable discretion. The Company's acceptance is based on, among other factors, conformance to the Platform's technical specifications, the Company's RAAS (Rules-as-a-Service) publish gates, brand voice standards, and applicable law.

1.3 **Ownership of Worker Content.** The Contributor's Worker **Ruleset**, prompts, configuration logic, and embedded domain knowledge (collectively, the "Worker Content") remain the intellectual property of the Contributor. The Company does not acquire ownership of the Worker Content under this Agreement; instead, the Contributor grants the Company a license to operate and commercialize the Worker on the Platform pursuant to Section 5 below. **This is a license, not an assignment.**

1.4 **Updates and Maintenance.** The Contributor is responsible for keeping each Worker reasonably current, including updates required to reflect changes in regulations, professional standards, or industry practice within the Worker's domain. The Company may, but is not obligated to, provide tooling or notifications to support such updates.

## 2. Revenue Share — Subscriptions

2.1 **Cash Share.** Subject to the terms of this Agreement, the Company will pay the Contributor **seventy-five percent (75%)** of the **Net Subscription Revenue** actually received by the Company from end users of each Worker authored by the Contributor.

2.2 **Net Subscription Revenue Defined.** "Net Subscription Revenue" means gross subscription fees collected by the Company that are directly attributable to a Contributor-authored Worker, less: (a) payment processor and platform fees (including but not limited to Stripe fees and applicable app-store fees, if any); (b) chargebacks, refunds, and credits issued to end users; (c) taxes collected on behalf of taxing authorities; and (d) currency conversion costs, where applicable.

2.3 **Payout Cadence.** Revenue share is paid **monthly**, in arrears, typically by the fifteenth (15th) day of the following calendar month. The Company will provide the Contributor with a statement for each payout cycle showing gross revenue, deductions, and the resulting payment amount.

2.4 **Minimum Payout Threshold.** No payout will be issued in a given cycle unless the Contributor's accrued and payable share equals or exceeds **fifty U.S. dollars ($50.00)**. Amounts below the threshold roll forward to subsequent cycles until the threshold is met.

2.5 **Bundled Workers.** Where a Contributor-authored Worker is included in a bundle, suite, or other multi-Worker offering, Net Subscription Revenue attributable to that Worker will be calculated using the Company's then-current bundle allocation methodology, applied consistently across all contributors in the bundle.

## 3. Inference and Data Fee Share

3.1 **Inference Credit Share.** The Company will pay the Contributor **twenty percent (20%)** of the net revenue derived from inference credits consumed by end users in sessions of Contributor-authored Workers, after deduction of underlying model and infrastructure costs.

3.2 **Data Pass-Through Share.** The Company will pay the Contributor **twenty percent (20%)** of the net markup revenue derived from third-party data pass-through fees billed to end users in connection with Contributor-authored Workers (including, where applicable, fees from external data providers such as property records, FAA data, vehicle history, identity verification, and similar services).

3.3 **Cadence and Statements.** Amounts payable under this Section 3 follow the same monthly payout cadence, minimum payout threshold, and statement requirements as set forth in Section 2.

3.4 **Cost Pass-Throughs Not Shared.** For clarity, the Contributor is entitled to a share of net margin only; the Contributor does not receive a share of amounts the Company remits to third-party model providers, data providers, or infrastructure vendors as direct cost of service.

## 4. Warrant Grant

4.1 **Grant.** Subject to the Contributor's execution of this Agreement and the Company's standard form of warrant certificate (the "Warrant Certificate"), the Company hereby grants the Contributor warrants to purchase **{{warrant_shares}}** shares of the Company's common stock (the "Warrants") at an exercise price of **$0.001 per share** (the "Strike Price").

4.2 **Important Distinction — Warrants vs. Equity.** The Warrants are a right to purchase shares of the Company's common stock at the Strike Price upon exercise. **The Warrants are not, and shall not be construed as, a present grant of equity, restricted stock, or RSUs.** Under SOCIII's Creator Equity v2 structure, present equity grants are reserved exclusively for designated advisors; contributors receive cash economics and warrants. The Contributor's status as a holder of unexercised Warrants does not confer voting rights, dividend rights, or any other rights of a stockholder until and unless the Warrants are properly exercised.

4.3 **Vesting Schedule.** The Warrants vest over a total period of **twenty-four (24) months** from the Effective Date, with a **six (6)-month cliff**. No Warrants vest prior to the six-month anniversary of the Effective Date; on that anniversary, 25% of the Warrant shares vest. The remaining 75% vest in equal monthly installments over the subsequent eighteen (18) months, subject to the Contributor's continuous service to the Company through each vesting date.

4.4 **Exercise Window Post-Termination.** Vested Warrants remain exercisable for **ninety (90) days** following termination of this Agreement for any reason. Unvested Warrants are forfeited upon termination. Warrants not exercised within the post-termination window expire and are forfeited.

4.5 **Change of Control Acceleration.** Upon a Change of Control of the Company (as such term is defined in the Warrant Certificate), **one hundred percent (100%) of the then-unvested Warrants** shall accelerate and vest immediately prior to the consummation of such Change of Control, provided that the Contributor has been in continuous service to the Company through the date of such Change of Control.

4.6 **HOM DAO pHOM Migration — Separate Calculation.** For Contributors who held **pHOM** (provisional HOM) tokens in HOM DAO and are migrating to the SOCIII platform under the Creator Equity v2 structure, the Warrant share quantity is sized to legacy pHOM holdings and is calculated separately, outside of the {{warrant_shares}} field above, pursuant to the migration schedule maintained by the Company. The legacy migration Warrants are governed by the same vesting, exercise, and acceleration terms set forth in this Section 4 unless expressly modified in the migration schedule.

4.7 **Securities Law Compliance.** The Warrants and any shares issued upon exercise are issued in reliance upon exemptions from registration under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended, and applicable state securities laws. The Contributor represents that the Contributor is acquiring the Warrants for investment purposes and not with a view to distribution.

## 5. Intellectual Property — License, Not Assignment

5.1 **Contributor Retains Ownership.** The Contributor retains all right, title, and interest in and to the Worker Content the Contributor authors, including the Ruleset, prompts, configuration logic, domain expertise, methodologies, and any underlying know-how. The Company acquires no ownership of the Worker Content under this Agreement.

5.2 **License Grant to Company.** The Contributor hereby grants the Company a **worldwide, perpetual, irrevocable (subject to Section 8 termination effects), royalty-free, fully paid-up, sublicensable, transferable license** to: (a) host, store, and execute the Worker Content on the Platform; (b) operate, display, and make the Worker available to end users; (c) market, promote, and distribute the Worker through the Marketplace and the Company's marketing channels; (d) sublicense the Worker to enterprise customers, resellers, and platform partners on terms consistent with the Company's then-current commercial practices; and (e) modify the Worker Content solely as necessary for technical compatibility, security, compliance, or to comply with applicable law.

5.3 **No Assignment of Underlying IP.** **For the avoidance of doubt, this Agreement does not transfer, assign, or convey ownership of the Worker Content, the Ruleset, the Contributor's underlying domain knowledge, or any patents, copyrights, trademarks, or trade secrets owned by the Contributor.** The Contributor is free to use the Worker Content and underlying knowledge in any context outside of the Platform, subject only to the confidentiality obligations in Section 7.

5.4 **Platform IP.** As between the Parties, the Company owns the Platform itself, including the runtime, the RAAS enforcement layer, the Marketplace, the user interface, analytics, telemetry, and all derivative data and metadata generated by the operation of the Worker on the Platform. Nothing in this Agreement grants the Contributor any rights in the Platform IP.

5.5 **Warranty of Originality.** The Contributor represents and warrants that the Worker Content is the original work of the Contributor or has been properly licensed by the Contributor from rights holders with authority to grant such licenses, and that the Contributor's grant of the license in Section 5.2 does not and will not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party.

## 6. Platform Compliance

6.1 **RAAS Publish Gates.** As a condition of publication and continued availability on the Marketplace, each Contributor-authored Worker must pass the Company's then-current RAAS publish gates, which may include identity verification of the Contributor, regulated-content review (e.g., for legal, financial, medical, or aviation domains), brand voice review, and any vertical-specific compliance gates the Company reasonably requires.

6.2 **Pause, Unpublish, or Remove.** The Company may, in its reasonable discretion, pause, unpublish, or remove a Worker from the Marketplace for material violations of platform rules, regulatory non-compliance, end-user safety concerns, or material defects affecting end-user experience. The Company will use commercially reasonable efforts to notify the Contributor in advance of any such action and to provide an opportunity to cure where practicable.

6.3 **Cooperation on Compliance.** The Contributor agrees to cooperate in good faith with reasonable Company requests related to compliance, incident response, end-user complaints, and regulatory inquiries pertaining to a Contributor-authored Worker.

## 7. Confidentiality

7.1 **Confidential Information.** Each Party may disclose to the other certain non-public information, including, on the Company's side, the Platform internals, user data, financial information, product roadmap, RAAS rule libraries, and proprietary data; and on the Contributor's side, the Worker Content and underlying domain methodologies designated as confidential (collectively, "Confidential Information").

7.2 **Obligations.** The receiving Party will: (a) use Confidential Information solely to perform under this Agreement; (b) protect it with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information of similar nature, and no less than a reasonable degree of care; and (c) not disclose it to any third party except to employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein.

7.3 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes public through no fault of the receiving Party; (b) was known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure without obligation of confidentiality; (c) is independently developed without reference to the disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction.

7.4 **Term.** The confidentiality obligations in this Section 7 survive termination of this Agreement for a period of **three (3) years**, except that obligations with respect to trade secrets continue for as long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

## 8. Term and Termination

8.1 **Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated in accordance with this Section 8.

8.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, for any reason or no reason, by providing the other Party with **thirty (30) days' prior written notice**.

8.3 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days after written notice; (b) becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy; or (c) engages in fraud, willful misconduct, or material violation of law in connection with this Agreement.

8.4 **Effect of Termination — Warrants.** Upon termination, **vested Warrants survive** subject to the ninety (90)-day post-termination exercise window in Section 4.4. Unvested Warrants are forfeited.

8.5 **Effect of Termination — Revenue Share.** Following termination, **the Contributor's revenue share under Sections 2 and 3 continues for so long as the Contributor's authored Worker remains available on the Platform** and generates qualifying revenue, subject to the Company's right under Section 6.2 to remove, pause, or unpublish a Worker. If a Worker is removed from the Platform following termination, the revenue share with respect to that Worker ceases at the time of removal, except for amounts accrued prior to removal.

8.6 **Survival.** The following sections survive termination: Section 1.3 (ownership of Worker Content), Section 5 (IP license, subject to revocation only as expressly provided), Section 7 (confidentiality), Section 8.4 and 8.5 (post-termination effects), Section 9 (independent contractor), Section 10 (representations), and Section 11 (miscellaneous).

9.2 **No Benefits.** The Contributor is not entitled to any employee benefits, including health insurance, retirement contributions, paid leave, or workers' compensation.

9.3 **Taxes.** The Contributor is solely responsible for all taxes, withholdings, and contributions owed in connection with payments received under this Agreement, including federal, state, local, and self-employment taxes. 9.4 **No Authority to Bind.** The Contributor has no authority to bind the Company to any contract, obligation, or representation, and shall not hold itself out as an employee, officer, or authorized agent of the Company.
