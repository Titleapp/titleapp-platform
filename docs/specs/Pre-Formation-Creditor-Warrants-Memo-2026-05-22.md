## ⚠ Eligibility Restriction — HOM DAO contributors

**Only HOM DAO contributors who put in $1,000 or more of direct cash count for warrant exposure.** The following do NOT qualify for warrant grants:

- Secondary-market PHOM token purchases (people who bought PHOM from other holders, not from the DAO directly).
- PHOM tokens issued *as a bonus alongside cash* — i.e., the PHOM portion of a mixed cash+token contribution does not separately qualify; only the underlying cash counts.
- Sub-$1,000 direct cash contributions — these were participation tokens at small scale, not creditor exposure that requires warrant remediation.

The HOM DAO contributor list will be regenerated against the original on-chain contribution records before warrants are issued. This rule scopes the warrant exposure tightly to material direct-cash supporters and avoids granting warrants to participants whose involvement was nominal or token-only.

## The Three-Tier Structure

Recognizing that not all exposures are morally equivalent — duration of patience, nature of the cash (loan vs equity-like contribution), and special circumstances (Robert's stolen funds) all matter — the warrants are tiered:

### Tier 1 — Single-Venture HOM DAO Contributors
**Applies to:** Eric Altshuler, Peter Farrley, Mike Lee, Robert's $75K HOM DAO portion
**Structure:** Warrants to purchase equity equal to **50% coverage of cash contribution at Series A valuation**
**Rationale:** These contributors took risk on one venture (HOM DAO) that didn't pan out commercially. 50% coverage means they're made whole on the original dollar if SOCIII exits at 2× the Series A valuation — a low bar that recognizes the patience without being either token or excessive.

### Tier 2 — Two-Venture Rollovers
**Applies to:** Anyone whose HOM DAO contribution rolled into TitleApp LLC (none on the current list; reserved as a category for completeness if more come to mind)
**Structure:** Warrants to purchase equity equal to **75% coverage of cash contribution at Series A valuation**

### Tier 3 — Three-Venture Rollovers (Patience Tier)
**Applies to:** Dan Bass, Stan Stalknaker, Tony Grenberg
**Structure:** Warrants to purchase equity equal to **100% coverage of cash contribution at Series A valuation**
**Rationale:** These three rolled their original RealEx investment through HOM DAO, through TitleApp LLC, and are now waiting on SOCIII. Full make-whole at Series A means they recover their original dollar if SOCIII hits Series A valuation, plus full upside from any growth above that point. This recognizes that patience-across-three-failures deserves more than a contributor who showed up once.

### Special Case — Loans
**Applies to:** Robert's $100K (stolen funds), Chris's $10K LBQ loan
**Structure:** Loans are formalized for repayment per separate agreements (Robert's already at 4% quarterly). PLUS a flat warrant grant equal to **25% of the loan amount at Series A valuation** as acknowledgment of risk taken.
**For Robert's stolen $100K specifically:** Sean's call on whether to apply the standard 25% loan-warrant grant or a more generous structure recognizing the third-party harm. Recommendation: **100% coverage on the stolen $100K** as a moral acknowledgment that the loss wasn't Robert's fault and wasn't even a venture loss in the normal sense — it was an external theft that the company couldn't prevent.

## Implementation Mechanics

### 1. Cap Table Representation

The warrants are issued by SOCIII Inc. (not by Sean personally) for two reasons:
- Cleaner tax treatment for the recipients (compensation income with established cost basis)
- Cleaner legal mechanic at exercise (company issues new shares to the warrant holder, not a personal transfer from Sean)

But Sean's founder allocation is sized down by the warrant pool size at the time of founder stock issuance, so the dilution effectively comes from his slice. This is documented in the founder stock issuance documents and the cap table itself shows:
- "Founder common stock: 48-53% (after pre-formation creditor warrant allocation)"
- "Pre-formation creditor warrants: 1.7% (allocated from founder)"

Institutional investors looking at the Series A cap table see a transparent structure with full disclosure.

### 2. Warrant Terms (Standard Across All Tiers)

- **Exercise price:** Set at Series A per-share price OR at the per-share price of the first priced equity round after grant, whichever comes first
- **Exercise window:** 7 years from grant date (longer than the 5-year advisor warrant window — these holders have been waiting longer)
- **Vesting:** Fully vested at grant (these aren't going-forward services, they're acknowledgment of historical contributions already made)
- **Acceleration on change of control:** Yes — if SOCIII is acquired before Series A, the warrants accelerate at the deal valuation
- **Transferability:** Permitted to immediate family, trusts for benefit of family, and inheritance. Not transferable to third parties without company consent (prevents secondary market in creditor warrants)
- **Cashless exercise:** Permitted (holder can elect to receive net shares without cash outlay at exercise)

### 3. Acceptance Flow (Reuses the Acceptance Architecture)

Each creditor goes through the same acceptance flow we're building for advisors:
1. Personalized email with deck explaining the structure
2. Magic-link landing page with three checkboxes:
   - "I acknowledge the cash exposure documented above and accept the proposed warrant structure"
   - "I agree to mutual release of any prior LLC member interest, DAO contributor claim, or RealEx-era token claim in exchange for this warrant grant"
   - "I will complete KYC/AML verification required by securities law before the warrant is formally issued"
3. After all three checks: warrant agreement delivered electronically; KYC handled inline via Stripe Identity

This produces a clean audit trail (per the Audit Trail patent filing) with the deck version hash, the terms version hash, the KYC attestation reference, and the acceptance event all bound together cryptographically.

### 4. Securities Law Compliance

The warrant grants qualify as private offerings under Reg D 506(b). Each recipient must be a verified accredited investor (most on this list are; verification is part of the KYC step). Mutual release language must be drafted by securities counsel — the goal is to extinguish any latent claim from the prior entities in exchange for the new warrant grant. The terms should be reviewed alongside the formal advisor agreements since several recipients (Robert, Eric, possibly Kim) are getting both advisor equity and creditor warrants and the documents should be coordinated.
