## Cofounder Advisor Agreement

This Cofounder Advisor Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of {{agreement_date}} (the "Effective Date") by and between **SOCIII, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 1810 E Sahara Avenue, Suite 75942, Las Vegas, NV 89104 (the "Company"), and **Kent Redwine**, an individual residing at {{advisor_address}} (the "Cofounder Advisor"). The Company and the Cofounder Advisor are referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

The Parties acknowledge that the Cofounder Advisor previously served as Chief Financial Officer of TitleApp LLC, a California limited liability company ("TitleApp LLC"), and that the predecessor business of TitleApp LLC has been or is being wound down in connection with the formation of the Company. This Agreement is intended to formalize the Cofounder Advisor's continuing role with the Company in connection with capital formation and the orderly transition of TitleApp LLC.

## 1. Services

(a) **Scope — Capital Formation.** The Cofounder Advisor shall provide capital-formation advisory services to the Company, including without limitation: (i) supporting the Company's preparation of investor-facing materials; (ii) identifying and introducing the Company to prospective investors within the Cofounder Advisor's professional network; (iii) advising the Company on round structure, terms, valuation, and investor relations; and (iv) participating, as reasonably requested by the Company, in investor meetings, diligence sessions, and post-investment communications (collectively, the "Capital-Formation Services").

(b) **Scope — TitleApp LLC Wind-Down.** The Cofounder Advisor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to complete, in cooperation with the Company and TitleApp LLC's other constituents, the orderly wind-down of TitleApp LLC, including the filing of a Certificate of Cancellation (or analogous document of dissolution) with the appropriate state authority, the settlement or papering of TitleApp LLC's outstanding obligations, and the closing of TitleApp LLC's books (collectively, the "Wind-Down Services").

(c) **No Other Services Required.** The Cofounder Advisor shall not be expected or required to perform services outside the scope of the Capital-Formation Services and the Wind-Down Services. The Parties acknowledge that the Cofounder Advisor's role is laser-scoped to capital formation and wind-down completion.

(d) **Manner of Performance.** The Cofounder Advisor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with prevailing industry standards. The Services shall be performed on an as-needed basis, with the Cofounder Advisor using commercially reasonable efforts to respond to inquiries from the Company within five (5) business days of receipt.

(e) **No Hour Commitment.** The Cofounder Advisor is not expected to dedicate any minimum number of hours per week or per month to the Company.

## 2. Compensation — Equity (Milestone-Vested)

(a) **Aggregate Grant.** Subject to approval by the Company's Board of Directors (the "Board") and the Cofounder Advisor's execution of the Company's standard form of Stock Option Agreement under the Company's equity incentive plan (the "Plan"), the Company shall grant to the Cofounder Advisor an option (the "Option") to purchase a number of shares of the Company's common stock equal to **fifteen percent (15.0%)** of the Company's fully-diluted common stock as of the Effective Date (the "Option Shares"). For purposes of this Agreement, "fully-diluted" means the total number of shares of common stock outstanding, assuming the conversion of all outstanding convertible securities, the exercise of all outstanding options and warrants, and the issuance of all shares reserved but unissued under the Plan.

(b) **Strike Price.** The exercise price per share of the Option shall be $0.001, which the Parties believe to be the fair market value of a share of the Company's common stock as of the Effective Date based on the Board's good-faith determination. If the Board determines a different fair market value at the time of grant, the exercise price shall be adjusted accordingly to reflect such determination and to comply with applicable tax law.

(c) **Milestone-Based Vesting.** The Option Shares shall vest in three (3) tranches, each equal to five percent (5.0%) of the Company's fully-diluted common stock as of the Effective Date, upon the occurrence of the following milestones (each, a "Vesting Milestone"):

  - **Tranche 1 — Wind-Down Completion.** Five percent (5.0%) shall vest in full upon written confirmation by the Company that the wind-down of TitleApp LLC has been substantially completed, including (i) the filing of a Certificate of Cancellation (or analogous dissolution document) with the appropriate state authority, and (ii) the closing of TitleApp LLC's books.

  - **Tranche 2 — Storyhouse Closing.** Five percent (5.0%) shall vest in full upon the closing of an equity or convertible-instrument financing of the Company in which **Storyhouse Ventures LLC** (or any successor or affiliated investment vehicle of Storyhouse Ventures LLC) participates as a lead, co-lead, or substantial participating investor.

  - **Tranche 3 — Additional Financing Closing.** Five percent (5.0%) shall vest in full upon the closing of any additional equity or convertible-instrument financing of the Company (other than the financing described in Tranche 2) in which the Company raises aggregate gross proceeds of at least two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($250,000), whether such financing is sourced by the Cofounder Advisor or by any other party.

(d) **No Time-Based Vesting; No Cliff.** The Option Shares shall not be subject to any time-based vesting schedule or cliff. The Option Shares shall vest, if at all, solely upon the occurrence of the Vesting Milestones described in Section 2(c).

(e) **Forfeiture on Termination.** Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, the Cofounder Advisor shall cease to vest in any Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has not yet occurred, and such unvested Option Shares shall be forfeited. Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has occurred prior to the effective date of termination shall remain exercisable in accordance with the terms of the Plan and the applicable Stock Option Agreement.

## 3. Compensation — Success Fee (Cash)

(a) **Success Fee on Sourced Capital.** In addition to the equity compensation described in Section 2, the Company shall pay to the Cofounder Advisor a cash success fee equal to **five percent (5.0%)** of the gross cash proceeds actually received by the Company in any equity or convertible-instrument financing in which the lead investor (or, in the case of a syndicated round without a single lead, the largest individual participating investor) was **Sourced by the Cofounder Advisor** (each, a "Qualifying Financing").

(b) **"Sourced by the Cofounder Advisor" Defined.** An investor shall be deemed "Sourced by the Cofounder Advisor" if, prior to the Company's first substantive engagement with such investor, the Cofounder Advisor (i) made a written introduction of such investor to the Company that was reasonably substantive in nature, and (ii) such introduction is acknowledged in writing by the Company. The Company shall acknowledge or decline each introduction within ten (10) business days of receipt.

(c) **Payment Timing.** The Success Fee, if earned, shall be paid in cash to the Cofounder Advisor within thirty (30) days following the closing date of the Qualifying Financing, by wire transfer or ACH to an account designated by the Cofounder Advisor.

(d) **Tail.** The Success Fee shall apply to any Qualifying Financing that closes during the Term and to any Qualifying Financing that closes within twelve (12) months following the termination of this Agreement, provided that the introduction giving rise to the Cofounder Advisor's entitlement was made during the Term.

(e) **Securities-Law Acknowledgment.** The Parties acknowledge that the Cofounder Advisor is not a registered broker-dealer under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. The Parties intend that the Cofounder Advisor's activities under this Agreement, and the payment of the Success Fee, shall comply with applicable securities laws and any available exemptions for non-registered finders. Each Party has had the opportunity to consult its own legal counsel with respect to the foregoing. If, at any time during the Term, the Company in good faith determines, on the advice of its securities counsel, that the payment of the Success Fee would create a material risk of violation of applicable securities laws, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to restructure the Success Fee in a manner that achieves the economic intent of this Section 3 while complying with applicable law.

## 4. Acceleration on Change of Control

Notwithstanding the milestone-based vesting set forth in Section 2(c), in the event of a Change of Control (as defined in the Plan, or if not defined in the Plan, as defined in customary single-trigger acceleration provisions for early-stage Delaware corporations) that occurs prior to the satisfaction of all Vesting Milestones, the Option Shares allocable to Tranche 3 of Section 2(c) (Additional Financing Closing) shall vest in full immediately prior to the consummation of such Change of Control. For the avoidance of doubt, no acceleration shall apply to Tranche 1 or Tranche 2 of Section 2(c) unless the corresponding Vesting Milestone has independently occurred.

## 5. Title and Public Representation

(a) **Cofounder Title.** The Cofounder Advisor may, with the Company's prior written consent (which the Company has granted concurrent with the execution of this Agreement), refer to himself externally as a "Cofounder" of the Company, including in his professional biography, LinkedIn profile, and similar venues.

(b) **No Officer or Director Authority.** The Cofounder Advisor shall not, by virtue of the "Cofounder" title or otherwise, hold any office, directorship, or other corporate authority within the Company unless separately and expressly appointed by the Board in writing. The Cofounder Advisor shall not bind the Company or incur any liability on behalf of the Company without the Company's prior written consent.

## 6. Independent Contractor Status

(a) **Relationship of the Parties.** The Cofounder Advisor is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, joint venturer, or agent of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment, partnership, joint-venture, or agency relationship between the Parties.

(b) **No Benefits.** The Cofounder Advisor shall not be eligible for, and shall not participate in, any employee benefit plans or programs of the Company, including without limitation health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off, or workers' compensation coverage (except as required by applicable law).

(c) **Taxes.** The Cofounder Advisor shall be solely responsible for the payment of all taxes, withholdings, and other governmental charges relating to the compensation paid or granted under this Agreement. The Company shall not withhold any amounts from the compensation paid to the Cofounder Advisor and shall report payments to the Cofounder Advisor on IRS Form 1099 or such other form as may be required by applicable law.

(d) **Control of Means and Methods.** The Cofounder Advisor shall control the means, methods, and manner of performing the Services, subject to the reasonable directions of the Company as to the desired outcome of the Services.

## 7. Confidentiality

(a) **Confidential Information.** "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by the Company to the Cofounder Advisor, whether orally, in writing, or by inspection of tangible objects, including without limitation business plans, product roadmaps, source code, technical specifications, customer and prospect lists, financial information, ruleset designs, model architectures, training data, partner relationships, fundraising materials, investor lists and pipeline, and any other information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential under the circumstances.

(b) **Obligations.** The Cofounder Advisor shall (i) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence, (ii) not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company, and (iii) not use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of the Services. The Cofounder Advisor shall protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that the Cofounder Advisor uses to protect his own confidential information of similar importance, and in no event less than reasonable care.

(c) **Exclusions.** The obligations in this Section 7 do not apply to information that the Cofounder Advisor can demonstrate by written records: (i) was in the Cofounder Advisor's possession without confidentiality obligation prior to disclosure by the Company; (ii) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Cofounder Advisor; (iii) is rightfully received by the Cofounder Advisor from a third party without confidentiality obligation; or (iv) is independently developed by the Cofounder Advisor without reference to or use of the Confidential Information. The Cofounder Advisor may also disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law or by a valid order of a court or governmental authority, provided that the Cofounder Advisor gives the Company prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation in seeking a protective order.

(d) **Survival.** The Cofounder Advisor's obligations under this Section 7 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years; provided, however, that with respect to any Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law, the Cofounder Advisor's obligations shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret.

## 8. Intellectual Property

(a) **Assignment.** The Cofounder Advisor hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest, including all intellectual property rights, in and to any and all work product, deliverables, ideas, concepts, inventions, improvements, designs, processes, methods, software, documentation, and other materials that the Cofounder Advisor conceives, creates, develops, or reduces to practice, alone or with others, in the course of performing the Services or that result from or relate to the Services (collectively, the "Work Product").

(b) **Pre-Existing IP.** The assignment in Section 8(a) does not extend to any intellectual property that the Cofounder Advisor owned, developed, or acquired prior to the Effective Date and that is listed on **Exhibit A** ("Pre-Existing IP"). If the Cofounder Advisor incorporates any Pre-Existing IP into the Work Product, the Cofounder Advisor hereby grants the Company a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive, sublicensable license to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and create derivative works of such Pre-Existing IP solely as incorporated in the Work Product and any natural extensions thereof.

(c) **Future Inventions.** With respect to any invention, work product, or intellectual property that the Cofounder Advisor conceives, creates, develops, or reduces to practice after the Effective Date that is not part of the Work Product and that does not result from or relate to the Services, the Cofounder Advisor retains all right, title, and interest, and nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as an assignment, license, or other transfer of such intellectual property to the Company.

## 9. No Conflict; Disclosure of Other Roles

(a) **No Conflicting Commitments.** The Cofounder Advisor represents and warrants that the Cofounder Advisor is not a party to any agreement, and is not subject to any obligation, that would prevent, restrict, or interfere with the Cofounder Advisor's ability to perform the Services or to grant the rights granted to the Company under this Agreement.

(b) **Disclosure of Other Roles.** The Cofounder Advisor shall promptly disclose to the Company any current or future board, advisor, consulting, or employment relationship with any company that is, or may reasonably be expected to become, a competitor of the Company. The Company acknowledges that the Cofounder Advisor may serve as an advisor, board member, or consultant to other companies, and that such service does not, in and of itself, constitute a breach of this Agreement so long as it does not materially conflict with the Services or the Company's interests.

(c) **No Use of Third-Party IP.** The Cofounder Advisor shall not use, in the performance of the Services or in any Work Product, any intellectual property of any third party (including any former or current employer of the Cofounder Advisor) without authorization to do so.

## 10. Term and Termination

(a) **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue until terminated in accordance with this Section 10 (the "Term").

(b) **Termination at Will.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, by giving the other Party thirty (30) days' prior written notice.

(c) **Effect of Termination on Vesting.** Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, the Cofounder Advisor shall cease to vest in any Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has not yet occurred, and such unvested Option Shares shall be forfeited. Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has occurred prior to the effective date of termination shall remain exercisable in accordance with the terms of the Plan and the applicable Stock Option Agreement.

(d) **Effect of Termination on Success Fee.** Termination of this Agreement shall not affect the Cofounder Advisor's entitlement to any Success Fee earned in respect of a Qualifying Financing introduction made prior to the effective date of termination, subject to the tail period set forth in Section 3(d).

(e) **Exercise Window.** The Cofounder Advisor shall have ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination to exercise any vested portion of the Option, after which any unexercised portion shall terminate and be forfeited, in each case subject to the terms of the Plan and any earlier expiration of the Option in accordance with its terms.

(f) **Survival.** The provisions of Sections 5 (Title and Public Representation), 6 (Independent Contractor Status), 7 (Confidentiality), 8 (Intellectual Property), 10(c)-(f) (Termination Effects and Survival), 11 (Miscellaneous), and any other provisions that by their nature should survive, shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## 11. Miscellaneous

(a) **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

(b) **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Plan, the Stock Option Agreement, and any exhibits hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, and communications, whether written or oral, relating to such subject matter.

(c) **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, legal, and enforceable, and the remaining provisions of this Agreement shall remain in full force and effect.

(d) **Counterparts; Electronic Signature.** This Agreement may be executed in two or more counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. The Parties agree that this Agreement may be executed by electronic signature, and that an electronic signature shall have the same legal effect as a manually executed original signature.

(e) **Amendment.** No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

(f) **Assignment.** The Cofounder Advisor may not assign this Agreement, in whole or in part, without the prior written consent of the Company. The Company may assign this Agreement to a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets without the consent of the Cofounder Advisor.

(g) **Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when delivered personally, sent by confirmed electronic mail, or sent by nationally recognized overnight courier, to the addresses set forth in the preamble or to the email address kent.redwine@gmail.com for the Cofounder Advisor, or to such other address as a Party may designate by written notice.

(h) **No Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any right or provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such right or provision or of any other right or provision.

(i) **Headings.** Section headings are for convenience of reference only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.
