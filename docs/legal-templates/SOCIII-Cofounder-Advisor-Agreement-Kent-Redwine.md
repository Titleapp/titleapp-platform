## Cofounder Advisor Agreement

This Cofounder Advisor Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of {{agreement_date}} (the "Effective Date") by and between **SOCIII, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 1810 E Sahara Avenue, Suite 75942, Las Vegas, NV 89104 (the "Company"), and **9th Element, Inc.**, a California corporation with a principal place of business at 2411 Chestnut Street, #205, San Francisco, CA 94123 (the "Advisor"). The Advisor's President, **Mr. Kent Redwine** ("Redwine"), will perform all Services (as defined below) on behalf of Advisor hereunder. Advisor and Redwine together are referred to as the "Service Providers." The Company, Advisor, and Redwine are referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

The Parties acknowledge that Redwine previously served as Chief Financial Officer of TitleApp LLC, a California limited liability company ("TitleApp LLC"), and that the predecessor business of TitleApp LLC has been or is being wound down in connection with the formation of the Company. This Agreement is intended to formalize Redwine's continuing role with the Company in connection with capital formation, investor relations, and the orderly transition of TitleApp LLC.

## 1. Services

(a) **Areas of Focus.** The Service Providers shall provide advisory services to the Company in the areas described in **Exhibit A** (the "Services"). For clarity, the Services are scoped to **capital formation and investor relations** activities and do not extend to other operational, strategic, or executive functions of the Company unless separately and expressly agreed in writing.

(b) **Capital-Formation Services.** Without limiting the foregoing, the Services shall include: (i) supporting the Company's preparation of investor-facing materials; (ii) identifying and introducing the Company to prospective investors within Redwine's professional network; (iii) advising the Company on round structure, terms, valuation, and investor relations; and (iv) participating, as reasonably requested by the Company, in investor meetings, diligence sessions, and post-investment communications.

(c) **TitleApp LLC Wind-Down (Legacy Scope).** Redwine shall also use commercially reasonable efforts to complete, in cooperation with the Company and TitleApp LLC's other constituents, the orderly wind-down of TitleApp LLC, including the filing of a Certificate of Cancellation (or analogous document of dissolution) with the appropriate state authority, the settlement or papering of TitleApp LLC's outstanding obligations, and the closing of TitleApp LLC's books (the "Wind-Down Services"). The Parties acknowledge that the Wind-Down Services arise from Redwine's prior role at TitleApp LLC and are included in this Agreement solely so that completion of those legacy duties may serve as a Vesting Milestone under Section 2(c).

(d) **Manner of Performance.** The Service Providers shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with prevailing industry standards. The Services shall be performed on an as-needed basis, and the Service Providers shall use commercially reasonable efforts to respond to inquiries from the Company within five (5) business days of receipt. The Service Providers are not expected to dedicate any minimum number of hours per week or per month to the Company.

## 2. Compensation — Equity (Milestone-Vested)

(a) **Aggregate Grant.** Subject to approval by the Company's Board of Directors (the "Board") and Redwine's execution of the Company's standard form of Stock Option Agreement under the Company's equity incentive plan (the "Plan"), the Company shall grant to **Redwine** (in his individual capacity, notwithstanding that Advisor is the contracting Service Provider) a non-statutory stock option (the "Option") to purchase a number of shares of the Company's common stock equal to **fifteen percent (15.0%)** of the Company's fully-diluted common stock as of the Effective Date (the "Option Shares"). For purposes of this Agreement, "fully-diluted" means the total number of shares of common stock outstanding, assuming the conversion of all outstanding convertible securities, the exercise of all outstanding options and warrants, and the issuance of all shares reserved but unissued under the Plan.

(b) **Strike Price.** The exercise price per share of the Option shall be $0.001, which the Parties believe to be the fair market value of a share of the Company's common stock as of the Effective Date based on the Board's good-faith determination. If the Board determines a different fair market value at the time of grant, the exercise price shall be adjusted accordingly to reflect such determination and to comply with applicable tax law.

(c) **Milestone-Based Vesting.** The Option Shares shall vest in three (3) tranches, each equal to five percent (5.0%) of the Company's fully-diluted common stock as of the Effective Date, upon the occurrence of the following milestones (each, a "Vesting Milestone"):

  - **Tranche 1 — Wind-Down Completion.** Five percent (5.0%) shall vest in full upon written confirmation by the Company that the wind-down of TitleApp LLC has been substantially completed, including (i) the filing of a Certificate of Cancellation (or analogous dissolution document) with the appropriate state authority, and (ii) the closing of TitleApp LLC's books.

  - **Tranche 2 — Storyhouse Closing.** Five percent (5.0%) shall vest in full upon the closing of an equity or convertible-instrument financing of the Company in which **Storyhouse Ventures LLC** (or any successor or affiliated investment vehicle of Storyhouse Ventures LLC) participates as a lead, co-lead, or substantial participating investor.

  - **Tranche 3 — Additional Financing Closing.** Five percent (5.0%) shall vest in full upon the closing of any additional equity or convertible-instrument financing of the Company (other than the financing described in Tranche 2) in which the Company raises aggregate gross proceeds of at least two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($250,000), whether such financing is sourced by Redwine or by any other party.

(d) **No Time-Based Vesting; No Cliff.** Except as provided in Sections 4 and 5 below, the Option Shares shall not be subject to any time-based vesting schedule or cliff. The Option Shares shall vest, if at all, solely upon the occurrence of the Vesting Milestones described in Section 2(c) or the acceleration events described in Sections 4 and 5.

(e) **Forfeiture on Termination.** Subject to Section 5 (Source-Acceleration Tail), upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Redwine shall cease to vest in any Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has not yet occurred, and such unvested Option Shares shall be forfeited. Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has occurred prior to the effective date of termination shall remain exercisable in accordance with the terms of the Plan and the applicable Stock Option Agreement.

## 3. Compensation — Success Fee (Cash)

> **[PENDING — Kent's counsel-supplied SEC-conforming language for the cash success fee will be integrated into this Section 3 in a subsequent revision. The provisions below reflect the Parties' commercial intent and are subject to refinement to ensure compliance with Section 15 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and applicable broker-dealer exemptions.]**

(a) **Success Fee on Sourced Capital.** In addition to the equity compensation described in Section 2, the Company shall pay to the **Advisor** (9th Element, Inc.) a cash success fee equal to **five percent (5.0%)** of the gross cash proceeds actually received by the Company in any equity or convertible-instrument financing in which the lead investor (or, in the case of a syndicated round without a single lead, the largest individual participating investor) was **Sourced by Redwine** (each, a "Qualifying Financing").

(b) **"Sourced by Redwine" Defined.** An investor shall be deemed "Sourced by Redwine" if, prior to the Company's first substantive engagement with such investor, Redwine (i) made a written introduction of such investor to the Company that was reasonably substantive in nature, and (ii) such introduction is acknowledged in writing by the Company. The Company shall acknowledge or decline each introduction within ten (10) business days of receipt.

(c) **Payment Timing.** The Success Fee, if earned, shall be paid in cash to Advisor within thirty (30) days following the closing date of the Qualifying Financing, by wire transfer or ACH to an account designated by Advisor.

(d) **Tail.** The Success Fee shall apply to any Qualifying Financing that closes during the Term and to any Qualifying Financing that closes within twelve (12) months following the termination of this Agreement, provided that the introduction giving rise to Advisor's entitlement was made during the Term.

(e) **Securities-Law Acknowledgment.** The Parties acknowledge that neither Advisor nor Redwine is a registered broker-dealer under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. The Parties intend that the activities under this Agreement, and the payment of the Success Fee, shall comply with applicable securities laws and any available exemptions for non-registered finders. Each Party has had the opportunity to consult its own legal counsel with respect to the foregoing. If, at any time during the Term, the Company in good faith determines, on the advice of its securities counsel, that the payment of the Success Fee would create a material risk of violation of applicable securities laws, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to restructure the Success Fee in a manner that achieves the economic intent of this Section 3 while complying with applicable law.

## 4. Acceleration on Change of Control or IPO

Notwithstanding the milestone-based vesting set forth in Section 2(c), in the event of (i) a Change of Control (as defined in the Plan, or if not defined in the Plan, as defined in customary single-trigger acceleration provisions for early-stage Delaware corporations), or (ii) an initial public offering of the Company's common stock ("IPO"), one hundred percent (100%) of the then-unvested Option Shares shall vest in full immediately prior to the consummation of such Change of Control or IPO.

## 5. Source-Accelerated Vesting (with Tail)

(a) **Source-Acceleration Trigger.** Notwithstanding the milestone-based vesting set forth in Section 2(c), if the Company accepts an equity or convertible-instrument investment (each, an "Investment") from any party that was identified, introduced, or substantively contacted by Redwine in the performance of the Services (a "Sourced Investment"), then one hundred percent (100%) of the then-unvested Option Shares shall vest in full immediately prior to the closing of such Sourced Investment. For the avoidance of doubt, this source-acceleration is independent of, and additive to, the Vesting Milestones described in Section 2(c) and the Change-of-Control/IPO acceleration described in Section 4.

(b) **Standard for "Sourced."** A party shall be considered "Sourced by Redwine" for purposes of this Section 5 using the same standard set forth in Section 3(b) (written, substantive introduction acknowledged in writing by the Company).

(c) **Source-Acceleration Tail.** If this Agreement is terminated by either Party prior to all Vesting Milestones being achieved, the source-acceleration rights described in this Section 5 shall remain effective for a period of **thirty-six (36) months** following the effective date of termination. During such tail period, the closing of any Sourced Investment shall cause all Option Shares that remained unvested as of the date of termination to vest in full immediately prior to the closing of such Sourced Investment.

(d) **Stacking with Success Fee.** The source-acceleration provided by this Section 5 is independent of, and additive to, the cash Success Fee provided by Section 3. A single Sourced Investment may give rise to both (i) the cash Success Fee payable to Advisor under Section 3, and (ii) source-acceleration of unvested Option Shares to Redwine under this Section 5.

## 6. Title and Public Representation

(a) **Cofounder Title.** Redwine may, with the Company's prior written consent (which the Company has granted concurrent with the execution of this Agreement), refer to himself externally as a "Cofounder" of the Company, including in his professional biography, LinkedIn profile, and similar venues.

(b) **No Officer or Director Authority.** Redwine shall not, by virtue of the "Cofounder" title or otherwise, hold any office, directorship, or other corporate authority within the Company unless separately and expressly appointed by the Board in writing. Neither Advisor nor Redwine shall bind the Company or incur any liability on behalf of the Company without the Company's prior written consent.

## 7. Independent Contractor Status

(a) **Relationship of the Parties.** Advisor and Redwine are independent contractors and not employees, partners, joint venturers, or agents of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment, partnership, joint-venture, or agency relationship between the Parties.

(b) **No Benefits.** Neither Advisor nor Redwine shall be eligible for, or shall participate in, any employee benefit plans or programs of the Company, including without limitation health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off, or workers' compensation coverage (except as required by applicable law).

(c) **Taxes.** Advisor shall be solely responsible for the payment of all taxes, withholdings, and other governmental charges relating to the compensation paid or granted under this Agreement. The Company shall not withhold any amounts from the compensation paid to Advisor and shall report payments to Advisor on IRS Form 1099 or such other form as may be required by applicable law.

(d) **Control of Means and Methods.** The Service Providers shall control the means, methods, and manner of performing the Services, subject to the reasonable directions of the Company as to the desired outcome of the Services.

## 8. Confidentiality

(a) **Confidential Information.** "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by the Company to the Service Providers, whether orally, in writing, or by inspection of tangible objects, including without limitation business plans, product roadmaps, source code, technical specifications, customer and prospect lists, financial information, ruleset designs, model architectures, training data, partner relationships, fundraising materials, investor lists and pipeline, and any other information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential under the circumstances.

(b) **Obligations.** The Service Providers shall (i) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence, (ii) not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company, and (iii) not use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of the Services. The Service Providers shall protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that they use to protect their own confidential information of similar importance, and in no event less than reasonable care.

(c) **Exclusions.** The obligations in this Section 8 do not apply to information that the Service Providers can demonstrate by written records: (i) was in their possession without confidentiality obligation prior to disclosure by the Company; (ii) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Service Providers; (iii) is rightfully received by the Service Providers from a third party without confidentiality obligation; or (iv) is independently developed by the Service Providers without reference to or use of the Confidential Information. The Service Providers may also disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law or by a valid order of a court or governmental authority, provided that they give the Company prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation in seeking a protective order.

(d) **Survival.** The Service Providers' obligations under this Section 8 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**; provided, however, that with respect to any Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law, the Service Providers' obligations shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret.

## 9. Intellectual Property

(a) **Assignment.** The Service Providers hereby irrevocably assign to the Company all right, title, and interest, including all intellectual property rights, in and to any and all work product, deliverables, ideas, concepts, inventions, improvements, designs, processes, methods, software, documentation, and other materials that the Service Providers conceive, create, develop, or reduce to practice, alone or with others, in the course of performing the Services or that result from or relate to the Services (collectively, the "Work Product"). The Service Providers agree to execute all applications for patents and/or copyrights, domestic or foreign, assignments, and other papers necessary to secure and enforce rights related to the Work Product.

(b) **Pre-Existing IP.** The assignment in Section 9(a) does not extend to any intellectual property that the Service Providers owned, developed, or acquired prior to the Effective Date and that is listed on **Exhibit B** ("Pre-Existing IP"). If the Service Providers incorporate any Pre-Existing IP into the Work Product, they hereby grant the Company a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive, sublicensable license to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and create derivative works of such Pre-Existing IP solely as incorporated in the Work Product and any natural extensions thereof.

(c) **Future Inventions.** With respect to any invention, work product, or intellectual property that the Service Providers conceive, create, develop, or reduce to practice after the Effective Date that is not part of the Work Product and that does not result from or relate to the Services, the Service Providers retain all right, title, and interest, and nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as an assignment, license, or other transfer of such intellectual property to the Company.

## 10. Non-Exclusivity

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary herein, nothing shall restrict, limit, or prevent the Service Providers from performing services similar in nature to the Services provided hereunder for any third party, both during and after the Term, provided that doing so does not (i) cause a breach of Section 8 (Confidentiality) or Section 11 (No Conflict; Disclosure of Other Roles) or (ii) materially conflict with the Services or the Company's interests.

## 11. No Conflict; Disclosure of Other Roles

(a) **No Conflicting Commitments.** The Service Providers represent and warrant that they are not a party to any agreement, and not subject to any obligation, that would prevent, restrict, or interfere with their ability to perform the Services or grant the rights granted to the Company under this Agreement.

(b) **Disclosure of Other Roles.** The Service Providers shall promptly disclose to the Company any current or future board, advisor, consulting, or employment relationship with any company that is, or may reasonably be expected to become, a competitor of the Company. The Company acknowledges that the Service Providers may serve as an advisor, board member, or consultant to other companies, and that such service does not, in and of itself, constitute a breach of this Agreement so long as it does not materially conflict with the Services or the Company's interests.

(c) **No Use of Third-Party IP.** The Service Providers shall not use, in the performance of the Services or in any Work Product, any intellectual property of any third party (including any former or current employer of Redwine) without authorization to do so.

## 12. Indemnification

The Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold the Service Providers harmless from and against (and the Company shall have the right to control, with attorneys of its choosing, the defense and settlement of) any and all claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or related to the Service Providers' performance of the Services and Redwine's affiliation with the Company, including in connection with any action, suit, proceeding, or any alternative dispute-resolution mechanism, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative, by reason of the fact that the Service Providers are or were performing Services under this Agreement on behalf of the Company or any subsidiary of the Company. Indemnified amounts shall include expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees of attorneys selected by the Company), judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement (provided that any settlement is approved in advance by the Company, which approval shall not be unreasonably withheld) actually and reasonably incurred by the Service Providers in connection with such action, suit, or proceeding. ## 14. Term and Termination

(a) **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue until terminated in accordance with this Section 14 (the "Term").

(b) **Termination at Will.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, by giving the other Party thirty (30) days' prior written notice.

(c) **Effect of Termination on Vesting.** Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Redwine shall cease to vest in any Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has not yet occurred, and such unvested Option Shares shall be forfeited, subject to the source-acceleration tail set forth in Section 5(c). Option Shares whose Vesting Milestone has occurred prior to the effective date of termination shall remain exercisable in accordance with the terms of the Plan and the applicable Stock Option Agreement.

(d) **Effect of Termination on Success Fee.** Termination of this Agreement shall not affect Advisor's entitlement to any Success Fee earned in respect of a Qualifying Financing introduction made prior to the effective date of termination, subject to the tail period set forth in Section 3(d).

(e) **Exercise Window.** Redwine shall have ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination to exercise any vested portion of the Option, after which any unexercised portion shall terminate and be forfeited, in each case subject to the terms of the Plan and any earlier expiration of the Option in accordance with its terms.

(f) **Survival.** The provisions of Sections 5(c) (Source-Acceleration Tail), 6 (Title and Public Representation), 7 (Independent Contractor Status), 8 (Confidentiality), 9 (Intellectual Property), 10 (Non-Exclusivity), 11 (No Conflict), 12 (Indemnification), 13 (Use of Name and Bio), 14(c)-(f) (Termination Effects and Survival), and 15 (Miscellaneous), and any other provisions that by their nature should survive, shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.
