## Section 2 — The Five Real AI Wins for Real Estate in 2026 (pages 4-6)

All quantitative claims given as **directional language** with sources where they exist:

### 2.1 Title chain analysis
- The job: reconstruct chain-of-title from county records, surface defects (break-in-chain, unreleased liens, missing assignments, forged signatures, encroachments)
- AI advantage: every public-record source pulled in seconds; pattern-matching against known fraud shapes
- What humans still need to do: legal interpretation, dispute resolution
- **Modeled benchmark:** substantial reduction in title-search hours; meaningfully more defects flagged than typical commitment

### 2.2 Zoning + permit + entitlement intelligence
- The job: not just "what zoning is this" but "what could this property be" — Sean's signature differentiator
- AI advantage: cross-jurisdictional zoning database, recent variance trends, environmental review triggers
- The pitch: "I want to convert this farmland to an urban city. The answer isn't no — the answer is here's the pathway."
- **Modeled benchmark:** tasks that took land-use consultants 20 hours done in 20 minutes with high accuracy on the strategic question

### 2.3 Comparable + market analysis with environmental risk overlay
- The job: comp pricing + flood/fire/earthquake/sea-level overlay + insurance availability projection
- AI advantage: environmental data layers + comp data + climate-adjusted insurance pricing trends
- **Modeled benchmark:** comp work that takes brokers half a day done in minutes; risk overlay catches what brokers miss

### 2.4 Escrow + closing workflow
- The job: doc preparation, condition tracking, missing-doc surfacing, closing-day timeline management
- AI advantage: every doc parsed, every condition tracked, every dependency chained
- **Modeled benchmark:** meaningful reduction in closing days, substantial reduction in last-minute scramble

### 2.5 Mortgage + lending pre-flight
- The job: borrower fit, document gathering, regulatory pre-screen (TILA, RESPA, fair-lending), rate competitiveness
- AI advantage: parses every doc + computes regulatory checks + optimizes for borrower's actual situation
- **Modeled benchmark:** meaningful reduction in time-to-clear-to-close; substantial reduction in fair-lending exposure

## Section 3 — The Five Failed AI Bets in Real Estate (page 7)

What hasn't worked despite hype:

1. AI valuation models marketed as appraisal replacements — appraisers are licensed; AI valuations supplement, never replace
2. AI-generated MLS listings — bot-written copy hurts SEO + buyer trust
3. AI lead-scoring with no human in the loop — fair-housing exposure when algorithm filters demographics
4. AI BDC for cold real-estate calls — TCPA exposure + terrible conversion vs. trained agents
5. "Smart contracts" for residential closings — premature; the legal infrastructure isn't there yet

The pattern: AI replacing licensed professional judgment fails. AI augmenting reading + comparing + pattern-matching wins.

### 5.2 The Gate Analogy (locked language — replaces technical RAAS description)

> Most AI tools work like an open door. You ask, the AI answers, and you're left to figure out if the answer is compliant, accurate, and defensible. SOCIII works like three gates.
>
> The first gate is at the entrance: regulatory rules are loaded into the worker's context as it begins thinking — like giving a securities attorney the rule book before they draft an offering memo, not after.
>
> The second gate checks the work: as the worker generates output, patterns that look like violations get flagged before the answer leaves the worker.
>
> The third gate is at the exit: the final output is screened for jurisdiction-aware compliance before it ever reaches you, your customer, or your regulator.
>
> Three gates, every output, every time. Tamper-evident audit trail at each gate. That's why SOCIII works in regulated industries where generic AI doesn't.

## Section 7 — How to Evaluate AI Tools for Your Real Estate Practice (page 13)

The 7-question checklist (mirror of Auto white paper but RE-flavored):

1. Audit trail — can you show it to your E&O carrier or state regulator?
2. Output rule-checking — does it block invalid outputs, or just generate confidently?
3. Multi-state compliance — does it know CA fair-housing differs from TX disclosure law?
4. Source citation — does it tell you which document said what, or just summarize?
5. Data ownership — yours, or the vendor's training set?
6. Fail-closed behavior — when uncertain, does it block or guess?
7. Licensed-professional integration — does it know when to hand off to your attorney?
